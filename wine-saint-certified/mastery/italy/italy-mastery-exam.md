- D) Recioto and Port are comparable in that both use 100% dried grapes , the difference is that Port dries grapes in the sun on slate terraces (the lagares), while Recioto dries grapes indoors on bamboo racks in ventilated fruttai; the sweetness level and alcohol content of both wines are identical

**Correct: B**

**Question 91**
Moscato d'Asti, Asti Spumante, and Brachetto d'Acqui are three distinct Piedmontese sparkling wines often confused with one another. What are the key differences a floor professional must know?

- B) Moscato d'Asti is frizzante (gently sparkling), very low in alcohol (typically 5–5.5% ABV), and intensely sweet: best for dessert or brunch; Asti Spumante is fully sparkling (spumante), slightly higher in alcohol (7–9% ABV) and somewhat less sweet than Moscato d'Asti: both are made from Moscato Bianco; Brachetto d'Acqui DOCG is made from the red Brachetto grape, producing a sweet, lightly sparkling red wine with vivid strawberry, rose, and raspberry aromatics , ideal with chocolate-based desserts and uniquely striking as a table presentation
- C) All three wines are produced from Moscato Bianco: the differences are entirely one of sparkling level (frizzante versus spumante versus pétillant) and aging time; Brachetto d'Acqui is simply Moscato d'Asti aged in contact with red grape skins to add color
- D) Brachetto d'Acqui and Asti Spumante are identical wines from different communes; Moscato d'Asti is a still wine that uses the same grape but is prohibited from having any effervescence under DOC regulations
