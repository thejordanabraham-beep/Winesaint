# Gabarinza: La Morra's Hidden Expression

Gabarinza is an MGA located within the commune of La Morra, the largest commune by vineyard area in the Barolo DOCG. The vineyard contributes to La Morra's reputation for producing some of Barolo's most elegant and aromatic wines.

## Geography and Exposition

Positioned on La Morra's hillsides at elevations between 280 and 350 meters above sea level, Gabarinza benefits from the favorable exposures typical of this commune. La Morra's position in the northwestern sector of Barolo creates distinctive growing conditions.

The vineyard enjoys good air circulation and the thermal variations that preserve freshness in the grapes.

## Soil and Geology

Gabarinza's soils share the Tortonian marl character typical of La Morra. These calcareous clay formations, known as Sant'Agata Fossil Marls, produce wines with pronounced perfume and silky tannins.

The relatively fertile soils create conditions that favor aromatic intensity and elegance over raw power.

## Wine Character

Wines from Gabarinza display La Morra's signature style: intense floral aromatics of roses and violets, bright red fruit notes, and delicate herbal undertones. The tannins are refined and silky, making these wines approachable earlier than Barolos from more structured communes.

These wines typically show beautifully from 8-12 years onward, with the capacity to evolve gracefully for 15-20 years. The finish shows subtle minerality and lingering floral notes.

## Producers

Dedicated producers work parcels in Gabarinza, crafting wines that honor La Morra's tradition of elegance. The site attracts winemakers who appreciate Nebbiolo's more perfumed expression.

## Historical Context

La Morra has long been recognized for producing Barolo of particular finesse. Gabarinza continues this tradition, offering authentic expression of the commune's distinctive character.
