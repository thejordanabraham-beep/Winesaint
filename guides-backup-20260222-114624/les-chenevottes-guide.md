# Les Chenevottes Premier Cru: A Complete Guide to Chassagne-Montrachet's Northern Jewel

## Overview & Location

Les Chenevottes stands as one of Chassagne-Montrachet's most distinguished Premier Cru vineyards, occupying a strategically positioned site on the northern slopes of this renowned commune in Burgundy's Côte de Beaune. Located in the Côte-d'Or département, this climat represents the sophisticated terroir expression that has made Chassagne-Montrachet one of Burgundy's most celebrated white wine appellations.

The vineyard sits in the northern sector of Chassagne-Montrachet, forming part of the commune's prestigious Premier Cru band that stretches across the hillside between 250 and 300 meters in elevation. This positioning places Les Chenevottes in direct proximity to some of Burgundy's most famous vineyard sites, creating a geographical context that underscores its importance within the Côte de Beaune hierarchy.

Les Chenevottes benefits from its location within the broader Chassagne-Montrachet appellation, which itself sits at the southern end of the Côte de Beaune, just north of the Côte Chalonnaise. This position creates a unique terroir bridge between the mineral-driven styles of Puligny-Montrachet to the north and the more structured, age-worthy expressions typical of Santenay to the south. The climat's northern exposure within Chassagne-Montrachet provides distinct advantages in terms of sun exposure and drainage, contributing to its reputation for producing wines of exceptional finesse and longevity.

The vineyard's boundaries are carefully delineated within the INAO (Institut National de l'Origine et de la Qualité) classification system, ensuring that only wines produced from this specific terroir can bear the Les Chenevottes Premier Cru designation. This precision in geographical definition reflects the centuries of viticultural experience that have identified this particular site as possessing unique characteristics worthy of Premier Cru status.

## Size

Les Chenevottes encompasses approximately 8.5 hectares (21 acres), making it a medium-sized Premier Cru within the Chassagne-Montrachet appellation. This size places it among the more substantial individual climats in the commune, providing sufficient scale for multiple producers to hold parcels while maintaining the terroir specificity that defines great Burgundy.

The vineyard's dimensions allow for meaningful variation within the climat itself, as different sections can express subtle terroir nuances based on their exact position, slope angle, and soil depth. This internal diversity contributes to the complexity that characterizes the finest expressions of Les Chenevottes, as producers with parcels in different sections of the vineyard often produce wines with recognizably distinct personalities while maintaining the overarching terroir signature of the site.

The relatively substantial size of Les Chenevottes also means that it supports a viable ecosystem of serious producers, from established négociant houses to small family domaines, each bringing their viticultural and winemaking philosophy to bear on this exceptional terroir. This diversity of approaches within a single climat creates a fascinating range of stylistic interpretations while highlighting the underlying terroir constants that define the site's character.

## Terroir & Geology

The geological foundation of Les Chenevottes rests on the characteristic Jurassic limestone formations that define the Côte de Beaune, specifically dating from the Bathonian and Bajocian periods of the Middle Jurassic era. The bedrock consists primarily of hard limestone mixed with marl, creating the mineral backbone that contributes to the distinctive character of wines from this climat.

The topsoil composition reveals a complex mixture of limestone-clay with significant presence of fossilized marine materials, reflecting the ancient seabed origins of this terrain. The clay content typically ranges from 25-35%, providing sufficient water retention for vine health while allowing adequate drainage to prevent waterlogging. This clay-limestone balance creates optimal conditions for Chardonnay cultivation, as the clay provides the richness and body while the limestone contributes the mineral precision and aging potential that characterize great white Burgundy.

Soil depth varies significantly across Les Chenevottes, ranging from relatively shallow sections where bedrock lies 30-40 centimeters below the surface to deeper pockets where topsoil extends to 80-100 centimeters. These variations in soil depth contribute to differences in vine vigor and fruit concentration, with the shallower soils typically producing more concentrated, mineral-driven wines, while deeper sections yield wines with greater richness and power.

The vineyard faces primarily east-southeast, providing optimal morning sun exposure while offering some protection from the harsh afternoon heat during summer months. This orientation ensures steady, gentle warming throughout the growing season, promoting even ripening while preserving the acidity levels essential for age-worthy Chardonnay. The slope angle ranges from 8-15%, providing excellent natural drainage while creating sufficient variation in sun exposure to add complexity to the terroir expression.

The elevation profile of Les Chenevottes, sitting between 250-285 meters above sea level, places it in the sweet spot of the Côte de Beaune hillside where drainage, sun exposure, and protection from weather extremes combine optimally. This elevation provides sufficient altitude to benefit from cooling breezes and diurnal temperature variation while remaining low enough to capture adequate warmth for full phenolic ripening.

Underlying geological faults and fractures in the limestone bedrock create natural drainage channels that help prevent water stagnation during wet periods while providing pathways for deep root penetration. These geological features contribute to the terroir's ability to produce consistently high-quality fruit even in challenging vintage conditions, as vines can access moisture and nutrients from deep within the bedrock structure.

## Climate & Microclimate

Les Chenevottes benefits from a continental climate tempered by maritime influences, creating growing conditions that favor the production of elegant, mineral-driven Chardonnay with excellent aging potential. The site's specific microclimate represents a refined expression of the broader Chassagne-Montrachet climate, with several unique characteristics that distinguish it from surrounding vineyards.

The east-southeast orientation creates a distinctive diurnal temperature pattern, with gentle morning warming followed by steady heat accumulation through midday, then gradual cooling as afternoon shadows begin to lengthen. This temperature profile promotes steady sugar accumulation while preserving the natural acidity that defines great white Burgundy, creating wines with both richness and freshness.

Protection from prevailing westerly winds comes naturally from the topographic features of the Côte de Beaune hillside, while the vineyard remains open to beneficial eastern breezes that help moderate temperature extremes and reduce humidity levels during critical periods of the growing season. This air circulation pattern significantly reduces disease pressure, particularly important for maintaining healthy Chardonnay fruit through the sensitive pre-harvest period.

The microclimate of Les Chenevottes shows distinct advantages during spring frost periods, as the sloped terrain and eastern exposure promote air drainage that carries cold air away from the vine canopy. This natural frost protection, combined with the moderating thermal mass of the limestone bedrock, helps protect against late spring freezes that can devastate Burgundy vineyards.

Rainfall patterns within the Les Chenevottes microclimate tend to follow regional norms, with approximately 750-800mm of annual precipitation distributed primarily in spring and fall months. However, the site's excellent drainage characteristics mean that excessive moisture rarely poses problems for vine health or fruit quality, while the limestone bedrock provides sufficient water retention to sustain vines during dry summer periods.

Summer heat accumulation follows a pattern that promotes steady ripening without excessive stress, with average growing season temperatures typically ranging 1-2 degrees Celsius below sites with southern or western exposures. This moderate heat accumulation contributes to the elegant, refined character that distinguishes Les Chenevottes from more powerful Premier Crus within Chassagne-Montrachet.

## Viticulture

Chardonnay dominates the plantings in Les Chenevottes, representing approximately 95% of the vineyard area, with small parcels of Pinot Noir accounting for the remainder. The overwhelming focus on Chardonnay reflects both the site's natural affinity for white varieties and market demand for premium white Burgundy from this celebrated terroir.

Vine age across Les Chenevottes varies significantly among different producers and parcels, with some sections containing vines planted in the 1960s and 1970s, while others feature more recent plantings from the 1990s and 2000s. The oldest sections, where present, typically produce the most concentrated and complex fruit, though many producers have found that vines reach optimal production quality after 15-20 years of age in this particular terroir.

Planting density follows traditional Burgundian practices, typically ranging from 9,000-12,000 vines per hectare depending on the specific section and producer preferences. Higher density plantings generally occur in areas with deeper soils, while sections with shallower bedrock often utilize slightly wider spacing to reduce competition among vines for limited soil resources.

Canopy management practices in Les Chenevottes must account for the site's natural vigor, which varies considerably based on soil depth and clay content. Producers typically employ careful shoot positioning and leaf removal strategies to optimize sun exposure while maintaining sufficient foliage to protect fruit from excessive heat. The timing of leaf removal becomes particularly critical, as the east-southeast exposure requires balance between morning sun penetration and afternoon protection.

Pruning practices generally follow the traditional Guyot system, with careful attention to bud selection and spur positioning to optimize fruit quality while managing yields. The natural fertility of the terroir requires disciplined yield management, with most quality-focused producers targeting yields between 45-55 hectoliters per hectare for optimal concentration and terroir expression.

Soil management approaches vary among producers, with many employing organic or biodynamic practices that emphasize soil health and natural balance. The limestone-clay composition responds well to minimal intervention approaches, though regular cultivation to manage weeds and improve soil structure remains common practice.

Harvest timing in Les Chenevottes typically occurs in mid-to-late September, depending on vintage conditions and producer style preferences. The site's natural acid retention allows for extended hang time when conditions permit, enabling producers to achieve full physiological ripeness while maintaining the freshness that defines great white Burgundy.

Disease pressure remains manageable due to the excellent air circulation and drainage characteristics of the site, though careful attention to canopy management and preventive treatments becomes essential during humid growing seasons. The limestone bedrock's natural pH buffering capacity helps maintain soil health and vine resistance to environmental stresses.

## Wine Character & Style

Les Chenevottes produces Chardonnay wines that exemplify the elegant, mineral-driven style that has made Chassagne-Montrachet famous worldwide. The wines consistently display a distinctive character that balances richness with finesse, power with precision, creating expressions that rank among Burgundy's finest white wines.

The aromatic profile of Les Chenevottes typically opens with pure citrus notes of lemon zest and grapefruit, layered with white flowers, particularly acacia and elderflower. As the wines develop, more complex aromas emerge including wet limestone, sea shells, and subtle herbs, while extended aging reveals honeyed notes, toasted hazelnuts, and delicate spice elements that speak to the terroir's complexity.

On the palate, Les Chenevottes demonstrates remarkable mineral precision, with a crystalline texture that carries flavors of citrus fruits, white peaches, and green apples. The limestone terroir contributes a distinctive chalky minerality that provides both textural interest and flavor complexity, while the clay component adds body and richness that supports the wine's structure.

The structural characteristics of Les Chenevottes include naturally high acidity that provides freshness and aging potential, typically ranging from 6.5-7.5 g/L depending on vintage conditions and winemaking approach. This acidity level perfectly balances the wine's natural richness, creating expressions that feel both substantial and lively on the palate.

Alcohol levels generally range from 12.5-13.5%, reflecting the site's ability to achieve full ripeness while maintaining elegance. The moderate alcohol contributes to the wine's overall balance and enhances its food-friendly character, while supporting the long-term aging potential that characterizes the finest expressions from this climat.

Textural qualities represent one of Les Chenevottes' most distinctive features, with wines displaying a unique combination of mineral tension and creamy richness. Young wines often show a tight, focused character that gradually opens to reveal layers of complexity, while mature examples develop a silky, almost viscous texture that speaks to the terroir's depth and complexity.

The finish of Les Chenevottes wines typically extends well beyond thirty seconds, with lingering mineral notes and subtle fruit flavors that evolve and develop in the glass. This persistence reflects both the quality of the terroir and the concentration achieved by careful viticulture and winemaking.

Vintage variation in Les Chenevottes tends to be more subtle than dramatic, with the terroir's natural balance helping to moderate extremes while allowing each year's unique character to express itself. Warm vintages tend to emphasize the richness and power, while cooler years highlight the mineral precision and aging potential.

## Comparison to Surrounding Crus

Within the Chassagne-Montrachet appellation, Les Chenevottes occupies a distinctive position that sets it apart from neighboring Premier Crus through its unique combination of power and finesse. Compared to Morgeot, the largest Premier Cru in the commune, Les Chenevottes typically shows greater mineral precision and aging potential, while Morgeot often displays more immediate richness and broader appeal.

The contrast with Caillerets, another prestigious Premier Cru in Chassagne-Montrachet, reveals Les Chenevottes' more structured, age-worthy character versus Caillerets' tendency toward elegance and perfume. Both sites produce exceptional wines, but Les Chenevottes generally requires more cellaring time to reach optimal drinking condition, while offering greater longevity potential.

Compared to Champs-Gain, Les Chenevottes demonstrates greater complexity and depth, with more pronounced mineral characteristics and superior aging capacity. The limestone influence appears more prominently in Les Chenevottes, contributing to its distinctive terroir signature and collector appeal.

The relationship to Montrachet, the famous Grand Cru that straddles the border between Chassagne-Montrachet and Puligny-Montrachet, reveals interesting parallels in terms of mineral intensity and aging potential, though Montrachet typically shows greater concentration and power. Les Chenevottes can be viewed as offering a similar stylistic direction with slightly less intensity but considerably more accessible pricing.

When compared to Premier Crus from neighboring Puligny-Montrachet, such as Folatières or Combettes, Les Chenevottes typically displays more body and richness while maintaining comparable mineral precision. The Chassagne-Montrachet terroir generally produces wines with greater weight and aging potential compared to the more ethereal style often associated with Puligny-Montrachet.

The contrast with Santenay Premier Crus to the south highlights Les Chenevottes' greater finesse and complexity, while Santenay wines often show more rustic character and earlier drinking appeal. This positioning reinforces Les Chenevottes' status as representing the refined expression of Chassagne-Montrachet terroir.

Domaine Marc Colin et Fils produces Les Chenevottes from their 0.4-hectare holding, with a winemaking approach that balances tradition and modernity. Bachelet-Monnot, a quality-focused domaine that has gained considerable recognition in recent years, produces Les Chenevottes from a small 0.15-hectare parcel. The stylistic variations among producers reflect different philosophies regarding oak usage, lees contact, and harvest timing, while the underlying terroir character of Les Chenevottes remains consistently recognizable across all expressions. This consistency speaks to the strength and distinctiveness of the terroir, while the variations demonstrate the creative possibilities within this framework.

## Historical Background & Classification

The history of Les Chenevottes extends back several centuries, with documented evidence of viticulture on this site dating to the medieval period when Burgundian monasteries first systematically developed the region's viticultural potential. The climat name itself derives from the Old French term referring to hemp cultivation, suggesting that this land served agricultural purposes long before its potential for grape growing was recognized.

The formal recognition of Les Chenevottes as a distinct vineyard site with superior quality potential occurred during the 18th and 19th centuries, as the reputation of Chassagne-Montrachet wines grew throughout European markets. Historical records from this period show that wines from Les Chenevottes commanded premium prices compared to village-level Chassagne-Montrachet, indicating early recognition of the site's exceptional terroir.

The classification of Les Chenevottes as Premier Cru occurred with the establishment of the AOC system in the 1930s, when French authorities formally codified the hierarchy of Burgundy vineyard sites based on centuries of empirical evidence regarding quality and consistency. The inclusion of Les Chenevottes in the Premier Cru classification reflected both its historical reputation and the consistent quality of wines produced from this terroir.

Throughout the 20th century, Les Chenevottes maintained its reputation as one of Chassagne-Montrachet's most reliable Premier Cru sites, with wines consistently showing the mineral precision and aging potential that define great white Burgundy. The climat survived the challenges of phylloxera, two world wars, and various economic pressures while maintaining its viticultural integrity and quality reputation.

Modern recognition of Les Chenevottes has grown significantly since the 1980s, as international appreciation for white Burgundy expanded and collectors began seeking wines that offered alternatives to the increasingly expensive Grand Cru vineyards. This growing recognition has led to increased investment in vineyard management and winemaking facilities, further enhancing the quality potential of this exceptional terroir.

The historical consistency of Les Chenevottes production has created a valuable database of vintage information that helps contemporary producers understand the site's behavior across different climatic conditions and viticultural approaches. This historical perspective informs current management decisions and contributes to the continuing evolution of quality from this distinguished climat.

## Aging Potential & Quality Level

Les Chenevottes produces wines with exceptional aging potential, typically reaching optimal drinking condition 5-8 years after vintage and maintaining quality for 15-20 years or more in favorable vintages. The combination of natural acidity, mineral concentration, and structural complexity provides the foundation for extended cellaring, while the terroir's inherent balance ensures graceful evolution over time.

Young wines from Les Chenevottes often display a tight, focused character that can seem austere in their first years, but this apparent restraint masks the complexity that emerges with proper cellaring. The development pattern typically shows initial fruit-driven aromatics giving way to more complex mineral and secondary flavors, while the texture evolves from crystalline precision to silky richness.

Quality consistency represents one of Les Chenevottes' greatest strengths, with even challenging vintages typically producing wines of Premier Cru caliber when proper viticultural and winemaking practices are employed. This reliability reflects both the terroir's natural balance and the accumulated experience of producers working with this site over generations.

The aging trajectory of Les Chenevottes follows a predictable pattern that varies primarily based on vintage conditions and winemaking style. Wines from cooler vintages tend to develop more slowly but often achieve greater complexity and longevity, while warmer years produce wines that show earlier but may have shorter optimal drinking windows.

Peak drinking periods for Les Chenevottes typically occur 8-12 years after vintage, when the wine's various components have integrated fully while retaining sufficient freshness and vitality. However, the finest expressions can continue evolving positively for two decades or more, developing the honeyed, nutty complexity that characterizes mature white Burgundy at its best.

Storage conditions significantly impact the aging potential of Les Chenevottes, with consistent cool temperatures and proper humidity being essential for optimal development. Wines stored under ideal conditions consistently outperform those subjected to temperature fluctuations or excessive heat, underscoring the importance of proper cellaring for realizing the full potential of this distinguished terroir.

The investment quality of Les Chenevottes has grown considerably as collectors have recognized the excellent value proposition it offers compared to Grand Cru vineyards, while the limited production and growing international recognition suggest continued appreciation potential for the finest expressions from this exceptional Premier Cru climat.
