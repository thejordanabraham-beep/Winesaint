# Les Aussy Premier Cru: A Complete Guide to Volnay's Distinguished Climat

## Overview & Location

Les Aussy stands as one of Volnay's most distinguished Premier Cru climats, positioned in the heart of the Côte de Beaune within Burgundy's prestigious wine region. This vineyard occupies a strategic location on the mid-slope of Volnay's amphitheater-like hillside, approximately 2.5 kilometers southwest of Beaune. The climat sits within the commune of Volnay, which forms part of the Côte de Beaune's southern sector, nestled between Pommard to the north and Meursault to the south.

The vineyard's position places it within the optimal viticultural zone of the Côte d'Or, where the interplay of elevation, exposure, and geological complexity creates ideal conditions for Pinot Noir cultivation. Les Aussy benefits from its location on the eastern-facing slope of the Côte de Beaune escarpment, which rises from the plains of the Saône Valley toward the Plateau de Hautes-Côtes. This positioning allows the vineyard to capture the gentle morning sun while being protected from harsh westerly winds by the higher elevations behind it.

The climat's boundaries are precisely delineated within the INAO (Institut National de l'Origine et de la Qualité) regulations, positioning it among the 29 Premier Cru climats officially recognized within Volnay. Its location represents the essence of Burgundian terroir expression, where centuries of viticultural refinement have identified this specific parcel as capable of producing wines of exceptional quality and distinctive character.

## Size

Les Aussy encompasses approximately 2.8 hectares (6.9 acres), making it a moderately-sized climat within Volnay's Premier Cru hierarchy. This relatively intimate scale is characteristic of Burgundy's fragmented vineyard structure, where small parcels allow for precise terroir expression and meticulous vineyard management. The compact size of Les Aussy ensures remarkable consistency across the climat while allowing individual producers to maintain distinct stylistic expressions within their specific parcels.

The vineyard's modest dimensions reflect the historical evolution of Burgundy's monastic and aristocratic land divisions, where plots were carefully delineated based on observed differences in soil, drainage, and exposition. This scale permits intensive hand cultivation and selective harvesting practices that are essential for maintaining the high quality standards expected from Premier Cru fruit.

## Terroir & Geology

The geological foundation of Les Aussy exemplifies the complex sedimentary history that defines the Côte de Beaune. The vineyard sits upon Middle Jurassic formations, primarily composed of Bathonian limestone dating back approximately 165 million years. This bedrock provides the fundamental mineral framework that influences both vine development and wine character.

The topsoil composition varies subtly across the climat, generally featuring a mixture of limestone-rich clay and weathered limestone debris. The soil depth ranges from 40 to 80 centimeters, with shallower sections occurring on the steeper portions of the slope where erosion has exposed more of the underlying bedrock. This variation in soil depth creates microenvironments within the vineyard that contribute to the complexity of wines produced from Les Aussy.

Clay content typically ranges between 25-35%, providing adequate water retention while maintaining sufficient drainage to prevent waterlogging. The presence of limestone fragments and pebbles throughout the soil profile enhances drainage and heat retention, creating favorable conditions for Pinot Noir ripening. The pH levels generally fall between 7.8-8.2, reflecting the calcareous nature of the terroir.

The vineyard faces primarily east-southeast, with slopes ranging from 8-12% gradient. This orientation ensures optimal sun exposure during morning and early afternoon hours while avoiding the potentially excessive heat of late afternoon sun. The elevation ranges from 260 to 290 meters above sea level, positioning Les Aussy within the ideal altitudinal band for premium Pinot Noir production in the Côte de Beaune.

Iron oxide presence in the soil contributes to the characteristic red-brown coloration found in certain sections of the vineyard, while limestone outcroppings provide additional mineral complexity. The terroir's drainage characteristics are enhanced by the natural slope and the presence of limestone fissures that allow excess water to percolate into the bedrock.

## Climate & Microclimate

Les Aussy benefits from a continental climate moderated by oceanic influences, typical of the Côte de Beaune region. The vineyard experiences warm, dry summers and cold winters, with annual rainfall averaging 750-800 millimeters. The growing season typically extends from April through October, with harvest usually occurring in late September or early October, depending on vintage conditions.

The east-southeast exposure creates a favorable microclimate that maximizes morning sun exposure while providing some protection from excessive afternoon heat. This orientation allows for gradual warming during the day, promoting steady photosynthesis and sugar accumulation without stress-inducing temperature spikes. The vineyard's position on the mid-slope creates natural air circulation patterns that help prevent frost damage in spring and reduce humidity levels during the growing season.

The elevation of Les Aussy provides cooler nighttime temperatures compared to vineyard sites closer to the valley floor, promoting acid retention and aromatic development in the grapes. Temperature variations between day and night during the ripening period typically range from 8-12°C, creating ideal conditions for phenolic maturity while maintaining freshness.

Protection from the Plateau de Hautes-Côtes moderates wind exposure, though sufficient air movement exists to reduce disease pressure. The vineyard's position also benefits from the thermal regulation provided by the limestone bedrock, which absorbs heat during the day and releases it gradually during cooler periods.

Rainfall distribution tends to be relatively even throughout the growing season, though summer drought periods can occur, stressing the vines sufficiently to concentrate fruit while the deep limestone bedrock provides access to underground water reserves when needed.

## Viticulture

Les Aussy is planted exclusively to Pinot Noir, reflecting the appellation regulations for red Volnay Premier Cru wines. The vineyard typically maintains vine ages ranging from 15 to 50 years, with some parcels containing older vines that contribute complexity and depth to the final wines. Planting density generally ranges from 10,000 to 12,000 vines per hectare, following traditional Burgundian practices that promote competition among vines and encourage deep root development.

Vineyard management in Les Aussy emphasizes sustainable practices, with many producers employing organic or biodynamic viticulture methods. The limestone-rich soils naturally regulate vine vigor, reducing the need for excessive chemical intervention. Pruning follows the Guyot system, typically single Guyot, which allows for precise crop load management and optimal fruit exposure.

Canopy management requires careful attention due to the site's favorable growing conditions, which can promote vigorous vegetative growth. Producers typically employ leaf removal, shoot positioning, and green harvesting to optimize sun exposure and air circulation while managing yields. Target yields generally range from 40-50 hectoliters per hectare, though quality-focused producers often harvest at lower yields to achieve greater concentration.

The vineyard's slope requires terracing in certain sections, with traditional Burgundian stone walls helping to prevent erosion while creating additional thermal mass for heat retention. Soil cultivation remains predominantly manual, using traditional tools to avoid compaction and preserve soil structure.

Site-specific viticultural challenges include managing vine vigor on the more fertile, deeper soil sections while ensuring adequate nutrition on the thinner, rockier portions of the vineyard. Harvest timing requires careful monitoring, as different sections of the climat may ripen at varying rates due to soil depth and exposure variations.

## Wine Character & Style

Wines from Les Aussy Premier Cru exhibit the refined elegance and complexity characteristic of superior Volnay terroir. The aromatic profile typically features red fruit dominance, with pure cherry, raspberry, and strawberry notes forming the core, complemented by subtle floral elements including violet and rose petal. As the wines develop, secondary aromatics emerge, including earth tones, forest floor, and delicate spice notes such as cinnamon and white pepper.

The palate demonstrates remarkable balance between power and finesse, with silky tannins that provide structure without overwhelming the fruit expression. The limestone influence manifests as a distinctive mineral backbone that supports the wine throughout its development. Acidity levels are typically well-integrated, providing freshness and aging potential while maintaining harmony with the fruit and tannin components.

Textural characteristics include a medium body with excellent concentration and a persistent finish that reveals layers of complexity. The mouthfeel exhibits the hallmark silkiness associated with quality Volnay, combined with sufficient grip to indicate serious aging potential. Color intensity ranges from medium ruby in lighter vintages to deeper garnet in more concentrated years.

The terroir's limestone foundation contributes a characteristic saline minerality that distinguishes Les Aussy from clay-dominant sites, while the east-southeast exposure preserves the fresh fruit character that defines exceptional Volnay. Wines typically display excellent clarity of expression, with terroir characteristics clearly discernible beneath the varietal fruit character.

Oak integration varies among producers, but the finest examples show judicious use of barrel aging that complements rather than dominates the terroir expression. The wines generally achieve phenolic maturity at moderate alcohol levels, typically ranging from 12.5-13.5% ABV, reflecting the site's ability to ripen grapes completely without excessive sugar accumulation.

## Historical Background & Classification

Les Aussy's viticultural history extends back to medieval times, when monastic communities first recognized the site's exceptional potential for grape cultivation. Historical documents from the 14th century reference vineyard plots in this area, indicating sustained viticultural activity over more than six centuries.

The climat's name derives from local toponymy, possibly relating to geographical or ownership characteristics that distinguished this section of the Volnay hillside. Throughout the ancien régime, the vineyard passed through various aristocratic hands, each contributing to the development of viticultural practices suited to the specific terroir.

The modern classification system that recognizes Les Aussy as Premier Cru was formalized through the INAO regulations established in the mid-20th century, building upon centuries of empirical observation regarding the site's wine quality. The 1936 Appellation d'Origine Contrôlée legislation provided the legal framework, while subsequent refinements in 1943 and later years precisely delineated the climat boundaries.

Recognition as Premier Cru status reflects not only the terroir's inherent quality but also the historical reputation of wines produced from this site. Throughout the 19th and early 20th centuries, Les Aussy wines were consistently recognized by négociants and wine merchants as superior examples of Volnay, commanding prices that reflected their quality level.

The climat survived the phylloxera crisis of the late 19th century, with replanting on American rootstocks maintaining viticultural continuity. Post-war developments in viticulture and winemaking have enhanced the expression of Les Aussy's terroir while respecting traditional practices that proved successful over centuries.
