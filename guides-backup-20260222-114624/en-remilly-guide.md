# En Remilly: A Premier Cru Vineyard Guide
## Saint-Aubin, Côte de Beaune, Burgundy

## Overview & Location

En Remilly stands as one of Saint-Aubin's most distinguished Premier Cru vineyards, representing the exceptional terroir diversity that has elevated this commune from relative obscurity to recognition as one of Burgundy's most compelling white wine appellations. Located in the heart of the Côte de Beaune, this Premier Cru climat occupies a strategic position within Saint-Aubin's viticultural landscape, contributing to the commune's reputation for producing wines that rival those of more celebrated neighboring appellations while maintaining their own distinct character.

The vineyard is situated in the commune of Saint-Aubin, positioned between the more famous wine villages of Puligny-Montrachet to the east and Chassagne-Montrachet to the southeast. This geographic placement places En Remilly within the golden triangle of Burgundy's greatest white wine production, benefiting from similar geological foundations and climatic conditions that have made the region synonymous with exceptional Chardonnay.

En Remilly's precise location within Saint-Aubin places it in the commune's southern sector, where the vineyard benefits from optimal exposition and protection from adverse weather patterns. The climat's boundaries are carefully delineated according to traditional Burgundian practices, with each parcel reflecting centuries of viticultural observation and land classification. The vineyard's position allows it to capture the essence of Saint-Aubin's terroir while expressing its own unique characteristics through the wines it produces.

The commune of Saint-Aubin itself occupies a somewhat hidden valley behind the more prominent slopes of Puligny and Chassagne-Montrachet, creating a natural amphitheater of vineyards that includes En Remilly among its premier sites. This positioning has historically provided both advantages and challenges, offering exceptional terroir conditions while remaining somewhat removed from the main wine routes that brought early recognition to neighboring appellations.

## Size

En Remilly encompasses approximately 4.2 hectares of vineyard land, making it a moderately sized Premier Cru within Saint-Aubin's portfolio of classified vineyards. This size places it among the mid-range Premier Cru climats in the commune, neither among the largest nor the smallest of Saint-Aubin's distinguished sites. The vineyard's size allows for meaningful production while maintaining the exclusivity and terroir expression that characterizes Premier Cru vineyards throughout Burgundy.

The 4.2-hectare surface area is distributed among multiple proprietors, following the traditional Burgundian pattern of vineyard ownership fragmentation that developed over centuries of inheritance practices and land transactions. This division results in various parcel sizes, with individual holdings ranging from small plots of less than half a hectare to larger sections approaching one hectare. The fragmented ownership pattern contributes to the diversity of winemaking approaches and styles that emerge from En Remilly, as different producers bring their own philosophies and techniques to their respective parcels.

Within the broader context of Saint-Aubin's Premier Cru vineyards, En Remilly's size positions it as a significant contributor to the commune's total Premier Cru production. Saint-Aubin boasts approximately 150 hectares of Premier Cru vineyard land across its various climats, making En Remilly's 4.2 hectares represent nearly three percent of the commune's classified vineyard surface. This proportion underscores the climat's importance within the appellation's hierarchy and its contribution to Saint-Aubin's overall reputation.

The vineyard's size also influences its market presence and availability. With production limited by the hectarage, En Remilly wines maintain a level of exclusivity that enhances their desirability among collectors and enthusiasts seeking authentic expressions of Saint-Aubin terroir. The moderate size ensures that while the wines are not as rare as those from tiny monopole vineyards, they retain the scarcity premium associated with Premier Cru classifications.

## Terroir & Geology

En Remilly's geological foundation represents a complex tapestry of sedimentary formations that define the character and potential of wines produced from this exceptional site. The vineyard sits upon the characteristic limestone-rich soils of the Côte de Beaune, specifically derived from Jurassic-era geological formations that have been shaped by millions of years of geological activity and erosion patterns unique to this region of Burgundy.

The dominant geological substrate consists primarily of Bathonian limestone, dating from the Middle Jurassic period approximately 165 million years ago. This limestone foundation provides the mineral backbone that contributes to the distinctive character of En Remilly wines, offering excellent drainage while maintaining sufficient water retention capacity during dry periods. The limestone's composition includes varying proportions of calcium carbonate and fossilized marine organisms, creating a complex mineral matrix that influences vine nutrition and ultimately wine flavor development.

Above the limestone bedrock, En Remilly's soils display the classic brown limestone composition typical of premier Côte de Beaune sites. The topsoil layer consists of clay-limestone mixtures with varying proportions of sand and small stone fragments, creating a heterogeneous growing medium that encourages deep root development and provides natural vine stress regulation. The soil depth varies across the vineyard, generally ranging from 40 to 80 centimeters before reaching the underlying limestone, with this variation contributing to the complexity and depth of character found in En Remilly wines.

The vineyard's soil composition includes significant proportions of small limestone pebbles and fragments, often referred to as "grès" in local terminology. These stone elements serve multiple terroir functions, improving drainage efficiency while providing thermal regulation through heat absorption and gradual release. During warm days, the stones absorb solar energy and release it slowly during cooler evening hours, creating favorable conditions for grape ripening and flavor development.

Slope orientation and gradient play crucial roles in En Remilly's terroir expression. The vineyard faces predominantly east and southeast, capturing optimal morning sunlight while benefiting from afternoon protection against excessive heat. The slope gradient, ranging from gentle to moderate depending on the specific parcel location, ensures proper drainage while preventing excessive erosion that could compromise soil structure and vine health.

The terroir's mineral composition extends beyond simple limestone foundations to include trace elements and micronutrients that influence vine development and wine character. Iron oxide deposits create subtle color variations in the soil, while clay minerals provide cation exchange capacity that affects nutrient availability. These geological nuances contribute to the complexity and distinctive character that distinguish En Remilly wines from those produced on different terroir types within Saint-Aubin and neighboring appellations.

## Climate & Microclimate

En Remilly benefits from a continental climate modified by oceanic influences, creating growing conditions that favor the production of elegant, mineral-driven white wines with excellent aging potential. The vineyard's position within the Côte de Beaune places it in a climatic zone characterized by warm summers, cool winters, and sufficient precipitation to support vine growth without excessive water stress during most growing seasons.

The vineyard's microclimate is significantly influenced by its topographical position within Saint-Aubin's natural amphitheater formation. The surrounding hills provide protection from harsh winds and extreme weather events while creating air circulation patterns that help prevent fungal diseases and promote healthy grape development. Cold air drainage during nighttime hours helps maintain temperature differentials that preserve acidity and aromatic complexity in the developing grapes.

Temperature patterns throughout the growing season play a crucial role in En Remilly's wine character development. Spring temperatures typically remain cool enough to prevent premature budbreak while allowing gradual vine awakening as daylight hours increase. Summer temperatures generally reach optimal levels for photosynthesis and sugar accumulation without excessive heat that could compromise acidity retention or aromatic development.

The vineyard's eastern and southeastern exposition maximizes beneficial morning sun exposure while providing afternoon protection during the hottest parts of summer days. This orientation allows grapes to achieve full phenolic ripeness while maintaining the fresh acidity that characterizes high-quality Saint-Aubin wines. The slope angle and direction also influence wind patterns, creating natural ventilation that reduces humidity levels and disease pressure.

Precipitation patterns significantly impact vintage variation in En Remilly wines. The site typically receives adequate rainfall during winter and spring months to replenish soil moisture reserves, while summer precipitation varies considerably between vintages. The limestone-rich soils provide excellent drainage during wet periods while retaining sufficient moisture during drought conditions, creating natural regulation that supports consistent vine performance across different climatic scenarios.

Autumn weather patterns prove particularly crucial for En Remilly's harvest timing and final wine quality. The vineyard's microclimate often extends the growing season through favorable September and October conditions, allowing extended hang time that enhances flavor concentration and aromatic complexity. Cool nighttime temperatures during the pre-harvest period help preserve acidity while allowing continued flavor development and phenolic maturation.

## Viticulture

Viticulture in En Remilly reflects both traditional Burgundian practices and modern refinements that optimize vine health and fruit quality while respecting the terroir's natural characteristics. The vineyard is planted exclusively to Chardonnay, following the Côte de Beaune tradition that recognizes this variety's exceptional affinity for limestone-rich terroirs and its ability to express site-specific characteristics with remarkable precision.

Vine density in En Remilly typically ranges from 10,000 to 12,000 vines per hectare, following Burgundian standards that promote competition among individual plants while ensuring adequate light exposure and air circulation. This density requires careful canopy management throughout the growing season to maintain optimal grape production without excessive vegetation that could compromise fruit quality or increase disease susceptibility.

Pruning practices follow the traditional Guyot system, with most producers employing simple Guyot configurations that limit production while encouraging concentrated fruit development. Winter pruning decisions significantly impact the following vintage's quality, with experienced vignerons adjusting bud counts based on vine vigor, previous year's production, and anticipated growing season conditions. The pruning philosophy emphasizes quality over quantity, typically targeting yields between 45 and 55 hectoliters per hectare to ensure optimal concentration and terroir expression.

Soil management practices vary among En Remilly's different proprietors but generally emphasize minimal intervention approaches that preserve soil structure and encourage natural vine-soil interactions. Many producers employ organic or biodynamic principles, avoiding synthetic chemicals and focusing on building soil health through cover crops, composting, and careful cultivation practices. Soil working typically occurs during winter and early spring months to avoid root damage and soil compaction during active growing periods.

Canopy management throughout the growing season requires careful attention to leaf positioning, shoot thinning, and grape cluster management. Leaf removal around grape clusters improves air circulation and reduces disease pressure while allowing optimal sun exposure for flavor development. Shoot positioning and tying ensure proper light penetration and support healthy photosynthesis without exposing grapes to excessive ultraviolet radiation or heat stress.

Harvest timing in En Remilly demands precise decision-making based on grape sampling and weather forecasting. Producers typically monitor sugar accumulation, acidity retention, and phenolic development through regular vineyard sampling, seeking optimal balance rather than maximum sugar levels. Hand harvesting remains standard practice, allowing careful grape selection and gentle handling that preserves fruit integrity from vine to winery.

Green harvesting and yield management practices help ensure optimal concentration and quality in En Remilly wines. Most producers perform selective cluster thinning during summer months, removing excess or poorly positioned grape bunches to concentrate the vine's energy into fewer, higher-quality clusters. This practice requires careful judgment to balance production levels with quality objectives while respecting the vine's natural vigor and growing conditions.

## Wine Character & Style

En Remilly produces Chardonnay wines that exemplify the elegance and mineral complexity characteristic of Saint-Aubin's finest Premier Cru sites while expressing distinctive characteristics that reflect the vineyard's unique terroir. These wines typically display a refined balance between fruit expression and mineral backbone, creating compelling examples of Côte de Beaune white wine artistry that can compete favorably with productions from more celebrated neighboring appellations.

The aromatic profile of En Remilly wines typically combines fresh citrus notes with more complex mineral and floral elements. Young wines often display vibrant lemon and grapefruit characteristics accompanied by white flower aromatics and subtle stone fruit nuances. As the wines develop, the aromatic complexity increases, revealing layers of honey, hazelnut, and brioche notes that emerge from careful aging and terroir expression.

On the palate, En Remilly wines demonstrate the characteristic mineral backbone derived from the vineyard's limestone-rich terroir. This mineral foundation provides structure and length while supporting fruit flavors that range from crisp apple and pear notes in younger wines to more complex honey and dried fruit characteristics in mature examples. The limestone influence creates a distinctive chalky texture that adds complexity without overwhelming the wine's inherent elegance.

Acidity levels in En Remilly wines typically remain well-preserved due to the vineyard's favorable microclimate and optimal harvest timing. This natural acidity provides freshness and aging potential while creating a framework that supports the wine's flavor development over time. The acid structure contributes to the wines' food-friendliness and enhances their ability to express terroir characteristics with clarity and precision.

The texture and mouthfeel of En Remilly wines reflect careful winemaking approaches that emphasize terroir expression while building complexity through appropriate aging techniques. Many producers employ partial barrel aging with judicious use of new oak to add textural richness without masking the vineyard's natural characteristics. The resulting wines typically display medium to full body with creamy textures that develop further complexity through bottle aging.

Vintage variation in En Remilly wines provides fascinating insights into the vineyard's terroir response to different climatic conditions. Cooler vintages tend to produce wines with heightened mineral expression and bright acidity, while warmer years result in richer, more opulent styles with enhanced fruit concentration. This vintage sensitivity demonstrates the terroir's responsiveness to climatic influences while maintaining consistent quality levels across different growing conditions.

The aging evolution of En Remilly wines reveals the full complexity potential of this exceptional terroir. Young wines typically display fresh fruit and floral characteristics supported by mineral undertones, while mature examples develop rich honey, nut, and spice notes that demonstrate the vineyard's capability to produce wines worthy of extended cellaring. The transformation process showcases both the terroir's inherent quality and the skill of producers who understand how to capture and preserve these characteristics through appropriate winemaking techniques.

The classification process involved extensive analysis of soil composition, microclimate conditions, exposition factors, and historical wine quality to determine which Saint-Aubin vineyards merited Premier Cru designation. En Remilly's inclusion among these distinguished sites reflects official recognition of its exceptional terroir and proven ability to produce wines of superior quality and distinctive character.

Throughout the post-classification period, En Remilly has continued to build its reputation through consistent quality production and increasing recognition among wine enthusiasts and critics.
