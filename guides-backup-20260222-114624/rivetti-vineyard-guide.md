# Rivetti MGA - Barbaresco

## A Note on This Site

Based on the comprehensive research data provided, **Rivetti does not appear as a recognized MGA (menzione geografica aggiuntiva) in Barbaresco**. The official MGA system, introduced in 2007 for Barbaresco, sensibly retained historically known vineyards and avoided absorbing them into more famous names for commercial reasons. However, Rivetti is not mentioned among the documented single-vineyard sites in any of the three Barbaresco communes.

The research data identifies the most recognized MGAs in Barbaresco as follows:

**In Barbaresco commune:** Asili, Montefico, Montestefano, Rabajà, San Lorenzo, Tildin, and Martinenga

**In Neive:** Albesani, Gallina, and Santo Stefano

**In Treiso:** Pajorè

## Possible Confusion

It is worth noting that **Rivetti is the surname of an important Barbaresco producer family** rather than a vineyard designation. The Rivetti family owns La Spinetta estate, which produces acclaimed Barbaresco wines from various vineyard holdings in the denomination. This may be the source of confusion, as producer names and vineyard names are sometimes conflated in wine discussions.

## The Barbaresco MGA System

The MGA system in Barbaresco predates Barolo's similar classification by three years, having been officially introduced in 2007. This list of single vineyards, also referred to as crus, represents an important refinement of the Piemontese DOC system and reflects decades of observation regarding which sites consistently produce distinctive, high-quality Nebbiolo.

The establishment of certain vineyards' prestige came from both historical recognition and the dedicated work of individual producers. As noted in the historical record, négociants' willingness to pay higher prices for grapes from certain vineyards established consensus about the finest sites. Some vineyards gained their current prestige specifically through the dedicated work and exacting standards of individual producers—San Lorenzo, Tildin, and Martinenga in Barbaresco, and Santo Stefano in Neive became renowned through the efforts of producers such as Gaja, Giacosa, and Alberto di Gresy.
