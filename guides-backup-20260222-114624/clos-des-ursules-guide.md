# Clos des Ursules: A Premier Cru Guide

## Overview & Location

Clos des Ursules stands as one of Beaune's most distinctive Premier Cru vineyards, occupying a privileged position within the Côte de Beaune's intricate mosaic of classified climats. Located in the heart of the Beaune appellation, this climat sits on the mid-slope section of the Côte d'Or escarpment, approximately 2.5 kilometers southwest of Beaune's historic city center.

The vineyard's strategic positioning places it within the golden amphitheater of slopes that have defined Burgundian viticulture for over a millennium. Clos des Ursules lies in the southern portion of Beaune's Premier Cru vineyard area, nestled between other renowned climats that collectively form one of Burgundy's most extensive Premier Cru zones. The site benefits from the protective influence of the Côte d'Or ridge to the west, which shields it from prevailing westerly winds while creating the optimal mesoclimate conditions that characterize this section of the Côte de Beaune.

Within the broader geographical context of the Côte d'Or, Clos des Ursules represents the transitional zone between the powerful, structured wines of the Côte de Nuits to the north and the more elegant, refined expressions typical of the southern Côte de Beaune. This positioning contributes significantly to the vineyard's unique character profile, combining elements of both sub-regions while maintaining its distinct terroir signature.

## Size

Clos des Ursules encompasses 2.27 hectares (5.61 acres) of Premier Cru vineyard land, making it a moderately sized climat within Beaune's Premier Cru hierarchy. This size classification places it among the mid-range Beaune Premier Crus, neither among the smallest boutique plots nor the most extensive vineyard areas within the appellation.

The compact nature of this climat allows for relatively homogeneous terroir characteristics across the entire vineyard area, though subtle variations in soil depth and composition create micro-zones that contribute to the wine's complexity. The vineyard's boundaries are clearly delineated, with the "clos" designation historically indicating an enclosed vineyard area, though modern boundaries may not perfectly reflect ancient stone walls that once defined the property.

## Terroir & Geology

The geological foundation of Clos des Ursules reflects the complex sedimentary history of the Côte d'Or, characterized by Jurassic limestone formations that define the region's Premier Cru terroirs. The vineyard sits primarily on Middle Jurassic Bathonian limestone, specifically the Comblanchien limestone formation that provides excellent drainage while retaining sufficient moisture for vine health during dry periods.

The soil profile reveals a sophisticated layering system typical of premier Côte de Beaune sites. The topsoil consists of 30-40 centimeters of brown limestone-clay mixture, with clay content ranging from 25-35% depending on the specific parcel location within the climat. This clay percentage provides optimal water retention capabilities while preventing waterlogging due to the underlying limestone bedrock's natural drainage properties.

Beneath the active soil layer, fractured limestone creates a complex network of fissures that allow vine roots to penetrate deeply, sometimes reaching 3-4 meters into the bedrock. This deep root penetration is crucial for accessing mineral nutrients and maintaining vine health during periods of water stress, contributing to the characteristic mineral backbone found in wines from this climat.

The vineyard's slope gradient averages 8-12%, creating excellent natural drainage while preventing excessive erosion. This moderate slope allows for optimal sun exposure without creating the extreme drainage conditions found on steeper Côte d'Or sites. The eastern exposure ensures morning sun capture while avoiding the most intense afternoon heat, contributing to the elegant style characteristic of Clos des Ursules wines.

Elevation ranges from 240-280 meters above sea level, positioning the vineyard in the optimal elevation zone for Premier Cru quality in this section of the Côte de Beaune. This elevation provides sufficient cooling influence to maintain acidity while ensuring adequate warmth for phenolic development in Pinot Noir.

## Climate & Microclimate

Clos des Ursules benefits from the classic continental climate of the Côte d'Or, modified by specific mesoclimate factors that distinguish it from surrounding climats. The vineyard experiences distinct seasonal variations, with cold winters, mild springs, warm summers, and extended autumns that provide optimal conditions for gradual grape ripening.

The eastern exposure creates a unique diurnal temperature pattern, with early morning sun exposure followed by afternoon shading from the higher slopes to the west. This exposure pattern moderates temperature extremes, preventing excessive heat accumulation while ensuring sufficient warmth for complete phenolic maturation. Morning dew evaporation occurs rapidly due to the eastern exposure, reducing fungal disease pressure compared to sites with delayed sun exposure.

Wind patterns play a crucial role in the vineyard's microclimate characteristics. The site's position provides protection from harsh western winds while allowing gentle air circulation that prevents stagnant air masses. This air movement is essential for maintaining vine health and preventing moisture-related diseases, particularly during humid periods in late summer and early autumn.

Rainfall distribution follows the typical Burgundian pattern, with approximately 700-800mm annually. The vineyard's slope and soil composition ensure rapid water drainage during heavy rainfall periods while the clay content provides sufficient water retention for dry summer months. This balance creates optimal water stress conditions that concentrate flavors without compromising vine health.

The frost risk in Clos des Ursules is generally moderate compared to lower elevation sites, though the eastern exposure can create vulnerability to late spring frosts when cold air settles in the valley. The slope gradient typically provides sufficient air drainage to minimize frost damage in most vintage conditions.

## Viticulture

Pinot Noir dominates the plantings in Clos des Ursules, representing approximately 95% of the vineyard area, with the remaining 5% planted to Chardonnay in specific parcels where soil composition and exposure create optimal conditions for white wine production. The Pinot Noir clones planted reflect both traditional Burgundian selections and more recent clonal research, with Pinot Fin representing the historical plantings and clones 114, 115, and 777 comprising more recent vineyard renovations.

Vine age varies significantly across the climat, with some parcels containing vines over 50 years old while replanted sections feature younger vines ranging from 10-25 years. The older vine sections typically produce the most complex and structured wines, with deep root systems that access the limestone bedrock's mineral resources.

Planting density follows traditional Burgundian practices, with approximately 10,000-12,000 vines per hectare depending on the specific parcel and planting date. This high density creates natural competition between vines, encouraging deep root development and concentrated fruit production essential for Premier Cru quality levels.

Viticultural practices in Clos des Ursules emphasize sustainable and traditional methods adapted to the specific terroir characteristics. Pruning follows the Guyot system, typically single Guyot for younger vines and double Guyot for mature plantings. Canopy management focuses on achieving optimal leaf-to-fruit ratios while maintaining adequate sun exposure for phenolic development.

Soil management varies among producers but generally emphasizes minimal intervention approaches. Many practitioners employ light tillage to encourage deep root development while maintaining soil structure. Organic matter incorporation typically occurs through compost application rather than synthetic fertilizers, supporting long-term soil health and microbial activity.

Harvest timing in Clos des Ursules typically occurs in mid to late September, depending on vintage conditions. The eastern exposure often results in slightly earlier ripening compared to western-facing sites, allowing for harvest scheduling that captures optimal phenolic maturity while preserving natural acidity.

Site-specific challenges include occasional spring frost risk, managing vine vigor on the richer soil sections, and preventing overcropping in high-yielding vintages. The clay content can create compaction issues if worked under wet conditions, requiring careful timing of viticultural operations.

## Wine Character & Style

Wines from Clos des Ursules exhibit a distinctive style that balances power and elegance, reflecting the climat's unique terroir characteristics. The Pinot Noir expressions typically display medium to medium-plus body with exceptional aromatic complexity and refined tannin structure that distinguishes them from both village-level Beaune and neighboring Premier Crus.

The aromatic profile frequently features primary fruit characteristics of red cherries, raspberries, and wild strawberries, often accompanied by floral notes of violet and rose petals. As the wines develop, secondary aromatics emerge including dried herbs, forest floor, and subtle spice elements derived from both terroir expression and potential oak integration. The mineral backbone, characteristic of the limestone terroir, manifests as a chalky or wet stone quality that provides structural foundation and aging potential.

Flavor development on the palate reveals layers of red fruit complexity, typically beginning with bright cherry and cranberry notes that evolve into deeper, more complex fruit expressions with aeration. The mid-palate shows the influence of the clay-limestone soils through a combination of fruit concentration and mineral texture that creates both richness and finesse.

Tannin structure in Clos des Ursules wines reflects the moderate slope and optimal sun exposure, producing tannins that are firm but approachable, with sufficient backbone for aging while avoiding the harsh astringency that can characterize wines from overly exposed sites. The tannins integrate well with the fruit components, creating a harmonious balance that supports both early consumption and long-term cellaring.

Acidity levels typically range from medium-plus to high, reflecting the continental climate and eastern exposure that preserves natural grape acidity during the ripening process. This acidity provides freshness and aging potential while contributing to the wines' food-friendly characteristics.

The textural qualities of Clos des Ursules wines often display a silky, refined mouthfeel with moderate weight and excellent length. The finish frequently reveals the terroir's mineral characteristics, with limestone-derived chalky notes and subtle earth tones that provide complexity and distinction.

## Comparison to Surrounding Crus

Clos des Ursules occupies a unique position within Beaune's Premier Cru hierarchy, displaying characteristics that distinguish it from immediate neighboring climats while sharing certain family resemblances with other mid-slope Beaune Premier Crus.

Compared to Les Grèves, located to the north, Clos des Ursules typically produces wines with more evident structure and mineral backbone, while Les Grèves wines often show greater immediate charm and accessibility. The soil composition differences contribute to this distinction, with Clos des Ursules' higher clay content providing more concentration and aging potential.

In relation to Clos du Roi, another prestigious Beaune Premier Cru, Clos des Ursules wines generally display more elegance and finesse, while Clos du Roi tends toward greater power and intensity. The exposure and elevation differences between these climats create distinct ripening patterns that influence the final wine styles.

When compared to Les Cent Vignes, located on similar mid-slope terrain, Clos des Ursules often shows more consistent quality across vintages due to its optimal exposure and drainage characteristics. Les Cent Vignes can produce exceptional wines in ideal vintages but may lack the reliability of Clos des Ursules in challenging weather conditions.

The wines from Clos des Ursules typically show more mineral precision compared to lower-slope Premier Crus like Les Reversées, which tend to display richer, more opulent fruit characteristics due to deeper, more fertile soils. Conversely, compared to higher-elevation sites like Les Marconnets, Clos des Ursules wines show greater fruit richness and approachability while maintaining excellent aging potential.

Within the broader Côte de Beaune context, Clos des Ursules wines occupy a middle ground between the powerful, structured wines of Pommard and the elegant, refined expressions of Volnay, combining elements of both styles while maintaining distinct terroir characteristics unique to this climat.

## Notable Producers

Louis Jadot holds the most significant portion of Clos des Ursules, with approximately 2.0 hectares under their control, making them essentially a monopole producer for this climat. Jadot's long-standing relationship with this vineyard has resulted in detailed terroir understanding and specialized viticultural practices adapted to the site's specific requirements.

Jadot's approach to Clos des Ursules emphasizes traditional Burgundian winemaking techniques with modern quality control measures. Their vineyard management focuses on sustainable practices, including organic soil amendments, careful canopy management, and hand harvesting at optimal ripeness levels. The winemaking typically involves traditional fermentation in open-top wooden vats with manual punch-downs, followed by aging in French oak barrels with approximately 30-40% new oak depending on vintage characteristics.

The remaining small parcels within the climat are owned by a few select producers who may vinify their fruit separately or sell to négociant houses. These smaller holdings sometimes produce distinctive interpretations that reflect individual winemaking philosophies while expressing the fundamental terroir characteristics of the climat.

Stylistic variations among producers, while limited due to the concentrated ownership, typically reflect different approaches to oak usage, extraction techniques, and aging regimens. Some producers emphasize the mineral aspects of the terroir through reduced oak influence and shorter macerations, while others may highlight the fruit concentration through extended skin contact and judicious use of new oak.

## Historical Background & Classification

The history of Clos des Ursules extends back to medieval times, with documented vineyard cultivation dating to at least the 12th century. The name "Ursules" derives from the Ursuline order of nuns who historically held vineyard properties in the Beaune region, though specific documentation of their ownership of this particular climat remains incomplete in historical records.

During the development of Burgundy's modern classification system, Clos des Ursules was officially recognized as a Premier Cru in the 1936 AOC legislation that established the hierarchical structure still used today. This classification recognized the climat's superior terroir characteristics and historical reputation for producing wines of exceptional quality and distinctive character.

The vineyard's reputation grew significantly during the 19th and early 20th centuries as Burgundy's wine trade expanded and quality consciousness increased. The site's consistent production of age-worthy wines with distinctive terroir expression established its reputation among both local producers and international wine merchants.

Throughout the 20th century, Clos des Ursules maintained its reputation as a reliable source of Premier Cru quality Pinot Noir, with wines that consistently demonstrated the balance of power and elegance that defines the best Beaune Premier Crus. The climat's relatively concentrated ownership has allowed for consistent viticultural management and quality maintenance across different vintage conditions.

## Aging Potential & Quality Level

Clos des Ursules produces wines with exceptional aging potential, typically reaching optimal maturity between 8-15 years after vintage, though the best examples can continue developing for 20-25 years or longer. The combination of natural acidity, structured tannins, and mineral backbone provides the foundation necessary for extended cellaring.

Young wines from this climat often display primary fruit characteristics with underlying structure that may seem austere initially. During the first 2-3 years after bottling, the wines typically show bright red fruit flavors with prominent tannins and high acidity. The integration period, usually occurring between years 4-8, sees the development of secondary aromatics and tannin softening that reveals the wine's true character.

Peak maturity generally occurs between years 8-15, when the wines achieve optimal balance between fruit, structure, and tertiary development. During this phase, Clos des Ursules wines display their characteristic combination of red fruit complexity, mineral precision, and silky texture that exemplifies Premier Cru quality from this climat.

Quality consistency remains high across different vintage conditions, though expression varies with weather patterns. Cool vintages tend to produce wines with more pronounced acidity and mineral character, requiring longer aging periods to achieve optimal balance. Warm vintages create wines with greater fruit concentration and earlier approachability while maintaining the structure necessary for extended aging.

The terroir characteristics of Clos des Ursules provide natural quality insurance through optimal drainage, moderate slopes, and ideal exposure that minimize vintage variation risks. Even in challenging weather conditions, the climat typically produces wines that meet Premier Cru standards, though stylistic variations reflect annual growing conditions.

Long-term aging reveals the true distinction of Clos des Ursules terroir, with mature wines displaying complex aromatics of dried flowers, forest floor, leather, and spice notes layered over a foundation of mineral-driven red fruit. The texture becomes increasingly silky and refined with age, while retaining sufficient structure to support continued development. These aged expressions demonstrate why Clos des Ursules ranks among Beaune's most respected Premier Cru climats, producing wines that embody both immediate pleasure and long-term collecting potential.
