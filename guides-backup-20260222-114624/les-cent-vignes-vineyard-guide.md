# Les Cent Vignes: A Comprehensive Guide to Beaune's Premier Cru Vineyard

## 1. Overview & Location

Les Cent Vignes stands as one of Beaune's most distinguished Premier Cru vineyards, representing the exceptional terroir that has made the Côte de Beaune renowned worldwide. Located in the heart of Burgundy's golden slope, this vineyard exemplifies the precise geographical and geological conditions that transform Pinot Noir grapes into wines of remarkable complexity and elegance.

Situated on the eastern-facing slopes of Beaune, Les Cent Vignes occupies a strategic position within the commune's Premier Cru hierarchy. The vineyard lies at the intersection of optimal elevation, ideal exposition, and exceptional soil composition that characterizes Burgundy's most celebrated wine-producing sites. The name "Les Cent Vignes," translating to "The Hundred Vines," reflects the historical practice of measuring vineyard plots by the number of vine stocks they could accommodate, though the modern vineyard encompasses considerably more than its literal translation suggests.

The vineyard's location places it within the broader context of Beaune's Premier Cru constellation, where it benefits from the same fundamental climatic conditions while expressing its own distinct terroir characteristics. This positioning allows Les Cent Vignes to capture the morning sun while receiving protection from harsh afternoon heat, creating an optimal environment for the slow, steady ripening that Pinot Noir demands.

The precise boundaries of Les Cent Vignes reflect centuries of viticultural evolution, where generations of winemakers have identified and delineated the specific parcels that consistently produce wines of superior quality. These boundaries are not arbitrary but represent the accumulated wisdom of centuries of observation regarding soil composition, drainage patterns, microclimate variations, and vine performance.

## 2. Size

Les Cent Vignes encompasses approximately 5.98 hectares (14.78 acres), making it a moderately-sized Premier Cru vineyard within Beaune's classification system. This size places it within the middle range of Beaune's Premier Cru holdings, neither among the largest like Les Grèves (31.6 hectares) nor among the smallest appellations that comprise less than two hectares.

The vineyard's dimensions allow for meaningful production volumes while maintaining the intimate scale necessary for meticulous viticulture. This size enables multiple producers to hold parcels within the vineyard, creating a diversity of winemaking approaches while working with fundamentally similar terroir conditions. The fragmented ownership structure typical of Burgundy means that Les Cent Vignes is divided among several domaines, each bringing their individual viticultural philosophy and winemaking techniques to their specific parcels.

The 5.98-hectare area represents planted vineyard surface, with the actual parcel boundaries carefully defined according to historical records and modern cadastral surveys. This precise demarcation ensures that only grapes grown within these specific boundaries can claim the Les Cent Vignes Premier Cru designation, maintaining the integrity and authenticity of the appellation.

The vineyard's size also influences production economics and market dynamics. With annual production typically ranging from 1,500 to 2,500 cases depending on vintage conditions and individual producer yields, Les Cent Vignes wines maintain relative scarcity in the global market while providing sufficient volume to establish recognition and develop collector interest.

## 3. Terroir & Geology

The geological foundation of Les Cent Vignes reveals the complex interplay of ancient marine deposits and subsequent geological evolution that characterizes the Côte de Beaune. The vineyard sits upon limestone bedrock from the Jurassic period, specifically the Bathonian and Bajocian stages, which provide the mineral backbone essential for premium Pinot Noir production.

The soil profile in Les Cent Vignes demonstrates the classic Burgundian combination of limestone subsoil overlaid with varying depths of clay-limestone topsoil mixed with rocky fragments. This composition ensures excellent drainage while retaining sufficient moisture during dry periods, creating the stress conditions that encourage vines to develop deep root systems and concentrate flavors in their grapes.

The topsoil depth varies across the vineyard, generally ranging from 30 to 60 centimeters, with areas of deeper soil occurring in natural depressions and shallower zones on more exposed slopes. This variation in soil depth contributes to the complexity found in Les Cent Vignes wines, as vines in different sections of the vineyard experience varying degrees of water stress and nutrient availability.

Iron oxide presence in the soil contributes distinctive mineral characteristics to the wines while supporting healthy vine nutrition. The oxidized iron content, visible in the reddish-brown coloration of certain soil sections, provides essential micronutrients and influences the wine's color development and tannic structure.

Limestone fragments throughout the soil profile serve multiple functions: they improve drainage, reflect sunlight to enhance ripening, and gradually release calcium and other minerals essential for vine health. The limestone's alkaline nature helps regulate soil pH, creating conditions favorable for beneficial soil microorganisms that support vine nutrition and terroir expression.

The geological complexity extends below the surface, where underground water circulation through limestone fissures creates a natural irrigation system that supplies vines with consistent moisture while preventing waterlogging. This subsurface water movement also facilitates mineral transport, enriching the soil solution that feeds the vine roots.

## 4. Climate & Microclimate

Les Cent Vignes benefits from the continental climate characteristic of the Côte de Beaune, modified by specific microclimate conditions created by its elevation, exposition, and topographical situation. The vineyard's eastern exposure captures optimal morning sunlight while receiving afternoon shade from the hills behind, creating a temperature moderation that extends the growing season and promotes gradual flavor development.

The elevation of Les Cent Vignes, ranging from approximately 240 to 280 meters above sea level, places it within the optimal altitudinal zone for Pinot Noir cultivation in Burgundy. This elevation provides sufficient warmth for ripening while maintaining the diurnal temperature variation essential for retaining acidity and developing complex aromatics.

Air circulation patterns within the vineyard contribute significantly to wine quality by reducing humidity levels and minimizing disease pressure. The natural airflow created by the valley's topography helps prevent the stagnant conditions that can promote fungal diseases, allowing for more sustainable viticulture with reduced intervention.

Rainfall patterns typical of the Côte de Beaune provide approximately 600-700mm annually, distributed throughout the growing season with peak precipitation often occurring during spring and autumn. The vineyard's slope and soil composition ensure proper drainage during heavy rainfall events while retaining sufficient moisture during dry summer periods.

The frost risk in Les Cent Vignes remains relatively low due to its elevation and air drainage characteristics. Cold air naturally flows downhill toward the valley floor, reducing the likelihood of damaging late spring frosts that can devastate lower-elevation vineyards. However, vintage variation in spring weather patterns can still create challenging conditions that require careful vineyard management.

Summer heat accumulation in Les Cent Vignes typically provides sufficient warmth for Pinot Noir ripening while rarely reaching excessive temperatures that might compromise wine quality. The combination of eastern exposure and afternoon shade creates optimal conditions for maintaining grape acidity while achieving phenolic ripeness.

## 5. Viticulture

Viticultural practices in Les Cent Vignes reflect both traditional Burgundian methods and modern sustainable techniques adapted to the specific requirements of this Premier Cru terroir. The vineyard's slopes necessitate careful consideration of erosion control, water management, and mechanical access while maintaining the intimate scale of cultivation that premium Burgundy demands.

Vine density in Les Cent Vignes typically ranges from 10,000 to 12,000 vines per hectare, following traditional Burgundian spacing that encourages competition among vines while maximizing terroir expression. This high density planting forces individual vines to develop deep root systems and limits per-vine yields, concentrating flavors and maintaining the intensity expected from Premier Cru fruit.

Training systems employed in the vineyard predominantly follow the Guyot method, either simple or double depending on individual producer preferences and specific parcel characteristics. This training approach allows for precise yield control while maintaining adequate leaf surface for photosynthesis and grape ripening. Canopy management throughout the growing season focuses on achieving optimal sun exposure for grape clusters while maintaining sufficient leaf coverage to prevent sunburn and excessive heat stress.

Soil management practices vary among producers but generally emphasize organic matter incorporation and minimal soil disturbance to preserve the natural soil structure and encourage beneficial microbial activity. Many growers have adopted organic or biodynamic practices, recognizing that chemical-free cultivation enhances terroir expression and long-term soil health.

Pruning techniques follow traditional Burgundian methods, with winter pruning designed to regulate yields and ensure optimal fruit quality. Summer pruning and leaf removal practices are carefully timed to expose grape clusters to morning sun while providing afternoon shade, promoting even ripening and maintaining natural acidity levels.

Harvest timing in Les Cent Vignes requires careful monitoring of grape maturity, balancing sugar accumulation with phenolic ripeness and acidity retention. Most producers conduct harvest manually using small picking containers to minimize grape damage and allow for field selection of the highest quality fruit.

## 6. Wine Character & Style

Les Cent Vignes produces Pinot Noir wines that exemplify the elegant, refined style characteristic of Beaune Premier Crus while expressing distinctive terroir-driven characteristics that set them apart from neighboring appellations. These wines typically display medium to medium-plus body with remarkable aromatic complexity and refined tannic structure.

The aromatic profile of Les Cent Vignes wines reveals layers of red fruit complexity, typically featuring cherry, raspberry, and strawberry notes in youth, evolving toward more complex expressions of dried fruits, earth, and mineral characteristics with age. The limestone-rich terroir contributes distinctive mineral undertones that provide backbone and structure to the wine's flavor profile.

Color intensity in Les Cent Vignes wines generally ranges from medium ruby to deep ruby, depending on vintage conditions and winemaking techniques. The wines maintain excellent color stability due to the terroir's ability to promote optimal phenolic ripeness while retaining natural acidity that preserves color compounds.

Tannic structure in these wines demonstrates the refined character typical of quality Burgundy, with tannins that integrate seamlessly into the wine's overall structure rather than dominating the palate. The limestone terroir contributes to this tannic refinement, producing wines with sufficient structure for aging while maintaining accessibility in their youth.

Acidity levels in Les Cent Vignes wines typically remain well-balanced, providing freshness and aging potential while supporting the wine's fruit characteristics. The terroir's ability to maintain natural grape acidity even in warm vintages contributes to the wines' reputation for consistency and ageability.

The mineral expression in Les Cent Vignes wines reflects the underlying limestone geology, manifesting as subtle chalky or stony notes that emerge more prominently with bottle age. This mineral character provides complexity and distinguishes these wines from fruit-driven expressions, adding layers of interest that reward careful tasting and extended cellaring.

Alcohol levels typically range from 12.5% to 14%, depending on vintage conditions and individual producer styles. The terroir's natural balance allows for complete ripening without excessive alcohol accumulation, maintaining the elegance and finesse that define quality Burgundy.
