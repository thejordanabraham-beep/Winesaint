## What the Research Data Reveals About the MGA System

The 2010 introduction of the Menzioni Geografiche Aggiuntive system formalized a classification that had existed informally for over a century. As the research notes, "certain privileged positions have long enjoyed greater prestige and given more distinctive wines in both the written tradition (from Lorenzo Fantini in the late 19th century to modern writers such as Luigi Veronelli, Renato Ratti and, more recently, Alessandro Masnaghetti) and in the oral tradition of the zone."

The MGA system operates on three levels within Barolo DOCG:
- Entire villages (such as La Morra or Verduno)
- Specified single vineyards (like Bussia or Cannubi)
- Multiple specified vineyards combined into a single MGA (as with the Via Nuova vineyard being included within the Terlo MGA)

Importantly, under current regulations, "the word *vigna* – vineyard – can now only appear as a labelling term if the name of the MGA is also stated."
