## Context: The Barbaresco MGA System

Understanding the broader MGA framework provides useful context. The *menzioni geografiche aggiuntive* system introduced in 2007 predated Barolo's similar classification by three years, representing an important step in codifying Barbaresco's single-vineyard tradition. As the research notes, single-vineyard bottlings in Barbaresco are "a relatively recent phenomenon, with the first, Prunotto's Barbaresco Montestefano, appearing in 1961."

Unlike Barolo, Barbaresco has a less firmly established written record of *cru* designation historically. Lorenzo Fantini's late 19th-century monograph on Piedmontese viticulture indicated "very few 'choice positions' in Barbaresco (and none whatsoever in Neive)." Systematic attempts to list and rate the finest positions only emerged in the 1960s with Luigi Veronelli and the 1970s with Renato Ratti.

The recognition of certain vineyards has often come through "négociants' willingness to pay higher prices for grapes from certain vineyards," establishing consensus about quality hierarchies. Additionally, some vineyards "have gained their current prestige from the dedicated work and exacting standards of producers such as Gaja, Giacosa, and Alberto di Gresy," demonstrating how producer reputation can elevate a site's standing.
