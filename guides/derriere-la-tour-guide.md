# Derrière la Tour Premier Cru: Saint-Aubin's Hidden Gem

## Overview & Location

Derrière la Tour stands as one of Saint-Aubin's most distinguished Premier Cru vineyards, occupying a privileged position within this commune that bridges the prestigious appellations of Puligny-Montrachet and Chassagne-Montrachet in Burgundy's Côte de Beaune. Located in the southeastern sector of Saint-Aubin's vineyard landscape, Derrière la Tour sits strategically positioned on the mid-slope of the commune's amphitheater-like topography, offering an ideal exposition and elevation that has earned it recognition among Burgundy's classified sites.

The vineyard's name, translating to "Behind the Tower," likely references historical landmarks that once dotted this landscape, though the precise origin remains embedded in local lore. What is certain is that this climat benefits from its proximity to some of Burgundy's most revered white wine territories while maintaining its own distinct character and terroir expression.

Saint-Aubin itself occupies a unique position in the Côte de Beaune hierarchy, serving as both a source of exceptional value and a showcase of terroir diversity. The commune's Premier Cru vineyards, including Derrière la Tour, demonstrate the complexity possible when ideal exposition, soil composition, and microclimate converge. The vineyard sits approximately 15 kilometers southwest of Beaune, nestled in the rolling hills that characterize this portion of the Côte de Beaune's southern extension.

## Size and Boundaries

Derrière la Tour encompasses approximately 2.8 hectares (6.9 acres), making it a moderately sized climat within Saint-Aubin's Premier Cru constellation. This size allows for meaningful production while maintaining the intimacy and terroir specificity that characterizes Burgundy's finest sites. The vineyard's boundaries are clearly delineated within the official INAO classification system, with precise cadastral references that have been refined through centuries of viticulture and formal recognition.

The climat's compact size contributes to its terroir homogeneity, as the entire vineyard experiences similar geological, topographical, and climatic conditions. This consistency is reflected in the wines' characteristic expression, allowing producers to capture the site's essence without the dilution that might occur in larger, more varied vineyard holdings.

## Terroir & Geology

The geological foundation of Derrière la Tour reveals the complex sedimentary history that defines the Côte de Beaune's terroir excellence. The vineyard sits atop Jurassic limestone formations, specifically the Bathonian and Bajocian stages that provide the mineral backbone for the commune's finest wines. The soil composition demonstrates the classic Burgundian combination of limestone bedrock overlain by varying depths of clay-limestone mixtures, with notable presence of marl deposits that contribute to both drainage and mineral complexity.

The topsoil depth varies across the vineyard, typically ranging from 30 to 80 centimeters, with the shallowest soils found on the upper portions where limestone bedrock approaches the surface. This variation in soil depth creates subtle differences in vine stress and mineral uptake, contributing to the complexity found in wines from this site. The clay content, averaging 20-25%, provides sufficient water retention for dry periods while allowing adequate drainage during Burgundy's often wet harvest seasons.

Derrière la Tour's slope characteristics are particularly favorable, with gradients ranging from 8-12% that provide natural drainage while preventing excessive erosion. The vineyard faces predominantly southeast, capturing crucial morning sunshine while benefiting from protection against the harsh western winds that can stress vines and impact fruit development. This southeastern exposition ensures optimal photosynthesis during the critical ripening period while maintaining the acidity retention essential for age-worthy wines.

The elevation ranges from approximately 280 to 320 meters above sea level, positioning the vineyard in the sweet spot between the valley floor's heavier, more fertile soils and the higher slopes where soil becomes too thin and harsh conditions can limit vine performance. This elevation provides the thermal variation necessary for complex flavor development while maintaining sufficient warmth for reliable ripening.

Geological analysis reveals significant presence of fossilized marine materials, remnants of the ancient Jurassic sea that once covered this region. These fossil deposits, including belemnites and various shellfish remains, contribute calcium carbonate and trace minerals that influence both soil pH and the mineral profile that eventually translates into wine character.

## Climate & Microclimate

Derrière la Tour benefits from a distinctive microclimate that combines Saint-Aubin's generally favorable conditions with site-specific advantages created by its topographical position. The vineyard experiences a continental climate with strong oceanic influences, typical of the Côte de Beaune, but its southeastern exposure and mid-slope position create particular microclimatic benefits.

The morning sun exposure is crucial for dew evaporation and disease prevention, while the site's position provides natural protection from the prevailing westerly winds that can cause vine stress and temperature fluctuations. The surrounding topography creates a natural amphitheater effect, concentrating warmth during the growing season while providing air circulation that prevents stagnant conditions conducive to fungal diseases.

Temperature moderation is a key characteristic of this site, with the limestone bedrock and elevated position preventing excessive heat buildup during summer while storing thermal energy that extends the growing season into autumn. Diurnal temperature variation averages 12-15°C during the ripening period, providing the thermal stress necessary for complex flavor compound development while preserving the acidity structure essential for longevity.

Rainfall patterns are influenced by the site's position relative to the surrounding hills, with natural drainage systems preventing waterlogging while the clay component in the soil provides adequate water retention during dry periods. The average annual precipitation of approximately 750mm is distributed favorably throughout the growing season, though vintage variation can significantly impact this pattern.

Wind patterns play a crucial role in the site's microclimate, with gentle air movement preventing moisture accumulation while avoiding the desiccating effects of stronger winds. The surrounding forest cover provides additional moderation, creating a buffer against extreme weather events while maintaining the air circulation necessary for healthy vine development.

## Viticulture

Derrière la Tour is planted predominantly to Chardonnay, which represents approximately 85% of the vineyard area, with the remaining 15% dedicated to Pinot Noir. This distribution reflects both the site's particular suitability for white wine production and market demand for Saint-Aubin's distinctive Chardonnay expressions.

The Chardonnay plantations typically utilize clones selected for their adaptation to limestone soils and moderate climate conditions. Common clonal selections include the 95, 96, and 548 clones, chosen for their ability to produce concentrated fruit while maintaining the acidity levels essential for Premier Cru quality. Vine age varies throughout the vineyard, with some parcels containing vines planted in the 1960s and 1970s, while more recent replanting has introduced younger vines using improved clonal material and rootstock combinations.

Planting density averages 10,000-12,000 vines per hectare, following traditional Burgundian practices that promote competition among vines and encourage deep root development. This density, combined with the limestone subsoil, forces roots to penetrate deeply, accessing mineral nutrients and water reserves that contribute to the wines' complexity and aging potential.

Viticultural practices in Derrière la Tour emphasize sustainable and often organic methods, reflecting the broader trend toward environmental consciousness among Burgundy producers. Canopy management is crucial given the site's fertility levels, with careful leaf removal and shoot positioning necessary to achieve optimal fruit exposure and air circulation. The southeastern exposure requires particular attention to avoid over-exposure during hot vintage years, while ensuring adequate photosynthesis for complete ripening.

Harvest timing is critical in Derrière la Tour, with the southeastern exposure typically advancing ripening by several days compared to north-facing sites. Producers monitor sugar accumulation, acidity retention, and phenolic ripeness carefully, often harvesting in multiple passes to ensure optimal fruit selection. The site's natural drainage allows for extended hang time in favorable vintages, contributing to the concentration and complexity that characterizes the best expressions from this climat.

Disease pressure management requires constant vigilance, particularly regarding powdery mildew and botrytis, though the site's air circulation and drainage characteristics provide natural protection compared to more enclosed or poorly drained locations. Integrated pest management approaches are standard, with many producers employing biodynamic principles to maintain soil health and vine balance.

## Wine Character & Style

Wines from Derrière la Tour display a distinctive character that combines Saint-Aubin's characteristic mineral precision with the depth and complexity expected from Premier Cru sites. The Chardonnay expressions typically exhibit a pale gold color with subtle green highlights in youth, evolving to deeper golden tones with age.

The aromatic profile is complex and layered, beginning with primary fruit expressions of white peach, pear, and citrus, particularly lemon and lime zest. As the wines develop in bottle, secondary aromas emerge, including honey, brioche, and subtle spice notes derived from oak aging and lees contact. The mineral component is pronounced, manifesting as crushed stone, wet limestone, and subtle saline notes that reflect the vineyard's geological foundation.

On the palate, Derrière la Tour Chardonnays demonstrate excellent balance between richness and freshness. The texture is typically medium to full-bodied, with a silky mouthfeel that reflects both the site's clay content and careful winemaking techniques. Acidity is prominent but well-integrated, providing structure and aging potential while maintaining immediate drinkability. The mineral backbone is substantial, creating a foundation that supports both fruit and oak integration.

Flavor development follows a progression from initial fruit intensity through mid-palate complexity to a finish that emphasizes minerality and subtle oak influence. The wines display excellent concentration without heaviness, achieving the balance that distinguishes Premier Cru quality from village-level wines. Typical alcohol levels range from 12.5-13.5%, reflecting the site's ability to achieve ripeness while maintaining elegance.

The Pinot Noir from Derrière la Tour, while less common, demonstrates the site's versatility. These wines typically show bright red fruit character with notable mineral influence, displaying medium body and excellent aging potential. The southeastern exposure contributes to reliable ripening while the limestone soils provide the structure necessary for complex development.

Oak integration varies among producers but typically emphasizes subtlety and support rather than dominance. The wines' mineral intensity and natural acidity structure allow for successful aging in both new and used barrels, with many producers utilizing 20-40% new oak to preserve the site's inherent character while adding complexity and aging potential.

## Comparison to Surrounding Crus

Derrière la Tour occupies a distinctive position within Saint-Aubin's Premier Cru hierarchy, offering characteristics that differentiate it from neighboring classified sites while maintaining the commune's overall style signature. Compared to Les Frionnes, located on the opposite side of the valley, Derrière la Tour typically produces wines with greater immediate accessibility and softer texture, reflecting differences in exposition and soil composition.

The contrast with En Remilly, Saint-Aubin's most prestigious Premier Cru, reveals Derrière la Tour's particular strengths. While En Remilly often displays greater power and aging potential, Derrière la Tour offers more immediate charm and approachability while maintaining substantial aging capacity. The southeastern exposure contributes to this accessibility, creating wines that are appealing in youth while developing complexity over time.

Relative to Sur le Sentier du Clou, another highly regarded Saint-Aubin Premier Cru, Derrière la Tour demonstrates greater consistency across vintages, benefiting from its favorable exposition and natural drainage. The mineral expression differs as well, with Derrière la Tour showing more integrated, subtle mineral notes compared to the sometimes more aggressive mineral character of higher-elevation sites.

When compared to the neighboring Chassagne-Montrachet and Puligny-Montrachet appellations, Derrière la Tour wines typically offer exceptional value while maintaining quality levels that approach these more prestigious communes. The style is generally less austere than Puligny-Montrachet and more mineral-focused than many Chassagne-Montrachet wines, occupying a stylistic middle ground that appeals to diverse palate preferences.

The site's unique character becomes most apparent when tasted alongside other Saint-Aubin Premier Crus in horizontal comparisons. Derrière la Tour consistently demonstrates excellent balance, with neither excessive richness nor overwhelming austerity, creating wines that reflect their terroir clearly while remaining approachable and food-friendly.

## Notable Producers

Several distinguished producers craft wines from Derrière la Tour, each bringing their individual winemaking philosophy to this exceptional terroir. Domaine Hubert Lamy stands among the most respected, holding significant parcels within the climat and consistently producing wines that showcase the site's potential. Their approach emphasizes minimal intervention viticulture and careful oak integration, resulting in wines that clearly express the vineyard's mineral character while developing complexity through extended lees aging.

Pierre-Yves Colin-Morey, though a relatively recent addition to the Saint-Aubin scene, has quickly established a reputation for exceptional Derrière la Tour wines. His meticulous attention to harvest timing and fermentation management produces wines of remarkable precision and aging potential. The use of carefully selected oak and extended lees stirring creates wines that balance immediate appeal with long-term development potential.

Domaine Marc Colin et Fils represents traditional excellence in Derrière la Tour, with family holdings dating back several generations. Their approach combines time-tested techniques with modern understanding of terroir expression, resulting in wines that consistently demonstrate the site's character across varying vintage conditions. Their parcels, located in the climat's central section, benefit from optimal exposition and soil composition.

Several négociant houses also produce wines from Derrière la Tour, though the quality varies significantly based on their fruit sourcing and winemaking approaches. The most successful négociant wines come from long-term relationships with growers and careful parcel selection, demonstrating that exceptional terroir can shine through various winemaking philosophies when treated with appropriate respect.

Emerging producers continue to recognize Derrière la Tour's potential, with younger vignerons increasingly focusing on this climat as they establish their reputations. These producers often bring fresh perspectives to the site while respecting its traditional character, creating dynamic tension between innovation and tradition that benefits consumers through diverse stylistic offerings.

## Historical Background & Classification

The history of Derrière la Tour reflects the broader development of Saint-Aubin as a recognized wine-producing commune within Burgundy's complex hierarchy. While viticulture in the region dates to medieval times, formal recognition of specific climats developed gradually through centuries of observation and quality assessment by local vignerons and merchants.

The climat received Premier Cru classification in 1970 as part of the comprehensive revision of Burgundy's appellation system. This recognition formalized what local producers had long understood: that Derrière la Tour possessed terroir characteristics worthy of classification above village level. The classification process involved detailed analysis of soil composition, exposition, historical performance, and wine quality, with Derrière la Tour meeting all criteria for Premier Cru status.

Historical documents from the 18th and 19th centuries reference the site's reputation for quality wine production, though detailed records are limited compared to more famous Côte de Beaune communes. Local archives indicate that wines from this area commanded premium prices relative to other Saint-Aubin sites, suggesting recognition of its superior potential even before formal classification.

The development of modern Saint-Aubin, including Derrière la Tour, accelerated in the post-World War II period as Burgundy underwent significant changes in ownership patterns and marketing approaches. The establishment of estate bottling among local producers allowed Derrière la Tour wines to develop individual reputations rather than disappearing into négociant blends, contributing to growing recognition of the site's distinctive character.

Recent decades have seen increased international attention focused on Saint-Aubin generally and Derrière la Tour specifically, as wine enthusiasts seek high-quality alternatives to the increasingly expensive and rare wines from neighboring prestigious communes. This attention has driven improvements in viticulture and winemaking while increasing market recognition of the site's exceptional value proposition.

## Aging Potential & Quality Level

Derrière la Tour produces wines with exceptional aging potential, typically reaching optimal maturity 8-12 years after vintage for Chardonnay and 10-15 years for Pinot Noir, though the best examples can continue developing for decades under proper storage conditions. The wines' aging trajectory follows a classic Burgundian pattern, with initial fruit-forward character gradually giving way to more complex secondary and tertiary aromas while maintaining structural integrity throughout their development.

Quality consistency is notably high across vintages, reflecting the site's favorable exposition and natural advantages. Even in challenging vintage years, Derrière la Tour typically produces wines that meet Premier Cru standards, though stylistic variations occur based on seasonal conditions. The southeastern exposure provides natural protection against many vintage pitfalls while the limestone-clay soils offer sufficient buffering capacity to maintain quality during weather extremes.

The evolution pattern typically begins with primary fruit dominance in the first 2-3 years, followed by integration of oak and development of secondary characteristics. By year 5-8, the wines enter their initial maturity phase, displaying optimal balance between fruit, mineral, and developed flavors. Peak drinking windows often extend from year 8-15, with the finest examples maintaining excellence well beyond this timeframe.

Vintage variation affects primarily the timing of development rather than ultimate quality potential. Warmer vintages tend to produce wines that develop more quickly but maintain longevity, while cooler years create wines requiring extended cellaring but often achieving greater complexity at maturity. The site's natural balance allows successful wine production across diverse vintage conditions, contributing to its reputation for reliability.

Quality levels consistently rank among Saint-Aubin's finest, with professional critics regularly scoring Derrière la Tour wines at levels comparable to much more expensive Côte de Beaune Premier Crus. The combination of terroir excellence, improved viticulture, and skilled winemaking has elevated the site's reputation significantly over recent decades, establishing it as a benchmark for Saint-Aubin Premier Cru quality while maintaining exceptional value relative to neighboring appellations.

The site's aging potential reflects fundamental terroir characteristics: sufficient acidity for preservation, mineral content for complexity development, and balanced extraction that provides structure without overwhelming tannins. These elements combine to create wines that reward patience while remaining accessible throughout their development, embodying the ideal balance sought in premium Burgundy.
