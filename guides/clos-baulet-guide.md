# Clos Baulet Premier Cru: A Comprehensive Guide to Morey-Saint-Denis' Hidden Gem

Clos Baulet stands as one of Morey-Saint-Denis' most distinctive Premier Cru vineyards, occupying a unique position within this prestigious commune of the Côte de Nuits. Located in the heart of Burgundy's golden slope, approximately 15 kilometers south of Dijon, this climat represents the sophisticated complexity that defines the village's winemaking heritage.

The vineyard sits strategically within Morey-Saint-Denis' Premier Cru hierarchy, positioned in the village's southern sector near the border with Chambolle-Musigny. This location places Clos Baulet within the transitional zone where the robust, structured character typical of northern Morey-Saint-Denis begins to show the more elegant, refined qualities associated with Chambolle-Musigny's influence. The climat's positioning along the mid-slope of the Côte de Nuits provides it with optimal exposure while maintaining the geological complexity that distinguishes Burgundy's greatest vineyard sites.

Within the broader context of the Côte de Nuits, Clos Baulet occupies a privileged position along the famous limestone escarpment that defines this renowned wine region. The climat's location places it within the sweet spot of elevation and exposure that characterizes the Côte de Nuits' most celebrated vineyard sites.

Clos Baulet encompasses approximately 2.4 hectares (5.9 acres), making it a moderately-sized Premier Cru within Morey-Saint-Denis' collection of classified vineyards. ## Terroir & Geology

The geological foundation of Clos Baulet reflects the complex sedimentary history of the Côte de Nuits, built upon Jurassic limestone formations that define the region's terroir character. The vineyard sits on predominantly Bajocian limestone, dating from the Middle Jurassic period approximately 170 million years ago. This limestone base provides excellent drainage while maintaining sufficient water retention for vine health during dry periods.

The topsoil composition varies across the climat, featuring a mixture of clay-limestone with varying proportions of iron-rich deposits and fossilized marine sediments. Clay content ranges from 20-35%, providing structure and mineral complexity while allowing for adequate drainage on the sloped terrain. The soil depth varies from 40-80 centimeters, with shallower soils on the upper portions of the vineyard and deeper deposits in the lower sections.

The vineyard's slope ranges from 8-12 degrees, creating natural drainage while providing multiple micro-exposures within the climat. This gentle gradient allows for optimal sun exposure without excessive erosion, maintaining soil integrity while ensuring proper water management. The aspect is predominantly east-southeast, providing morning sun exposure that gently warms the vines while protecting them from the harshest afternoon heat.

Elevation within Clos Baulet ranges from approximately 280-320 meters above sea level, positioning the vineyard within the optimal altitude band for Pinot Noir cultivation in Burgundy. This elevation provides sufficient temperature moderation to maintain acidity while ensuring adequate warmth for proper phenolic ripening. The varied elevation across the climat contributes to harvest complexity, with different sections reaching optimal ripeness at slightly different times.

The geological substrate includes significant amounts of limestone gravel and small rock fragments, contributing to the wine's mineral backbone and providing additional drainage. Iron-oxide deposits, evident in the reddish coloration of certain soil sections, contribute to the wine's color intensity and add subtle spice notes to the flavor profile. The presence of fossilized oyster shells and other marine deposits reflects the ancient oceanic origin of these soils, adding layers of mineral complexity to the terroir expression.

## Climate & Microclimate

Clos Baulet benefits from a continental climate moderated by the protective influence of the Côte de Nuits' forested hills. The vineyard experiences warm, dry summers with cool nights that preserve acidity, while relatively mild winters minimize frost damage risk. Annual precipitation averages 700-800mm, with most rainfall occurring during winter and spring months when vines are dormant or in early growth phases.

The climat's east-southeast exposure creates a favorable microclimate that captures morning sunlight while providing afternoon shade protection during the hottest part of the day. This exposure pattern allows for gradual, even ripening while maintaining the fresh acidity that characterizes high-quality Pinot Noir. The gentle slope promotes air circulation, reducing humidity and minimizing disease pressure during the growing season.

Temperature variations within the vineyard reflect its elevation changes and varied exposure angles. Upper sections tend to experience slightly cooler nighttime temperatures due to elevation, while lower areas benefit from marginally warmer conditions that can advance ripening. These micro-variations contribute to the complexity of fruit that emerges from different vineyard blocks.

The vineyard's position relative to surrounding forests provides protection from harsh winds while allowing beneficial air movement. Morning mists from the nearby valleys provide humidity during early growing season, supporting initial vine development before dissipating as temperatures rise. This natural moisture regulation contributes to stress management during critical growth periods.

## Viticulture

Clos Baulet is planted exclusively to Pinot Noir, following the traditional pattern of red wine production that defines most of Morey-Saint-Denis' Premier Cru vineyards. The vineyard features a mixture of vine ages, with some sections containing 40-60 year old vines that provide concentration and complexity, while younger plantings of 15-25 years contribute freshness and vitality to the blend.

Planting density ranges from 10,000-12,000 vines per hectare, following Burgundian traditions that emphasize competition among vines to concentrate flavors while maintaining manageable yields. This high density requires careful canopy management to ensure adequate air circulation and light penetration, particularly important given Pinot Noir's susceptibility to fungal diseases.

Viticultural practices in Clos Baulet emphasize sustainable, terroir-focused approaches that maximize site expression. Most producers employ organic or biodynamic methods, using natural preparations and minimal interventions to maintain soil health and vine balance. Pruning follows traditional Burgundian methods, typically utilizing the Guyot system that allows for precise yield control while maintaining vine structure.

Yield management is crucial in Clos Baulet, with most producers targeting 35-40 hectoliters per hectare to ensure concentration and complexity. Green harvesting in July and August removes excess clusters, allowing remaining fruit to achieve optimal ripeness and intensity. Leaf removal is carefully timed to provide fruit exposure without causing sunburn or excessive dehydration.

Harvest timing requires careful attention to phenolic ripeness rather than simply sugar accumulation. The varied elevation and exposure within the climat often necessitates multiple picking passes to capture fruit at optimal maturity levels. Most producers hand-harvest with small picking bins to minimize fruit damage and maintain quality.

Site-specific challenges include managing the varied soil depths and drainage characteristics across the vineyard. Upper sections with shallow soils require careful moisture management during dry periods, while lower areas with deeper soils may need drainage attention during wet seasons. The clay component in certain sections can create compaction issues that require regular soil cultivation.

## Wine Character & Style

Wines from Clos Baulet exhibit a distinctive character that reflects both the climat's unique terroir and its position within Morey-Saint-Denis' stylistic spectrum. The wines typically display medium to medium-full body with elegant structure, showing more refinement than power compared to some of the commune's more robust Premier Crus.

The aromatic profile commonly features red fruit characteristics including cherry, raspberry, and strawberry, often accompanied by floral notes of rose and violet that reflect the vineyard's elegant terroir expression. As the wines develop, secondary aromatics emerge including forest floor, mushroom, and subtle spice notes that add complexity without overwhelming the primary fruit character.

Mineral expression is a defining characteristic of Clos Baulet, with limestone-derived notes providing backbone and longevity. This minerality appears as wet stone, chalk, and subtle saline qualities that enhance the wine's overall complexity and food-pairing versatility. The iron content in certain soil sections contributes subtle metallic and spice notes that add interest without dominating the profile.

Structurally, Clos Baulet wines demonstrate well-integrated tannins that provide framework without excessive astringency. The tannin quality tends toward fine-grained and silky, supporting the fruit without creating harsh or drying sensations. Acidity is typically vibrant and well-balanced, providing freshness and aging potential while maintaining harmony with the fruit and alcohol levels.

The textural qualities of Clos Baulet wines often show remarkable elegance, with a silky, refined mouthfeel that distinguishes them from more muscular Morey-Saint-Denis expressions. This textural refinement reflects both the terroir characteristics and the careful viticultural practices employed in the vineyard.

Color intensity varies with vintage conditions and winemaking approaches but generally shows good concentration without excessive extraction. The wines typically display attractive ruby-red hues with garnet highlights that deepen with age, reflecting both the terroir's iron content and the quality of fruit achieved in the vineyard.
