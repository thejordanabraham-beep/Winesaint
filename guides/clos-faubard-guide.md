# Clos Faubard Premier Cru: Santenay's Hidden Jewel

## Overview & Location

Clos Faubard stands as one of Santenay's most distinguished Premier Cru vineyards, occupying a privileged position within this southernmost commune of the Côte de Beaune. Located in the heart of Burgundy's golden slope, approximately 40 kilometers south of Dijon and just north of the Côte Chalonnaise, Santenay represents the final chapter of the Côte de Beaune's prestigious wine story, and Clos Faubard serves as one of its most eloquent expressions.

The vineyard sits on the eastern slopes above the village of Santenay, nestled within a natural amphitheater that has been shaped by millennia of geological forces. This Premier Cru occupies the middle section of Santenay's slope, positioned strategically between the village vineyards below and the forest-crowned heights above. The climat benefits from an ideal southeastern exposure, capturing the morning sun while remaining protected from harsh western winds by the ridge that forms the backbone of the Côte de Beaune.

Clos Faubard's location within Santenay places it at the intersection of several geological and climatic influences that converge to create its unique terroir. The vineyard lies approximately 15 kilometers south of Chassagne-Montrachet and 12 kilometers from Meursault, sharing certain geological characteristics with these more famous communes while maintaining its own distinct personality. The proximity to the Saône River valley to the east moderates temperature extremes, while the protective influence of the Côte's escarpment provides shelter from adverse weather patterns.

The vineyard's position represents the culmination of the Jurassic limestone formations that define the Côte de Beaune, where the geological strata reach their southern terminus before transitioning into the different terroir characteristics of the Côte Chalonnaise. This geographical positioning imbues Clos Faubard with both the nobility of its famous northern neighbors and the approachable charm that characterizes southern Burgundy.

## Size

Clos Faubard encompasses approximately 2.84 hectares (7.02 acres), making it a moderately sized Premier Cru within Santenay's hierarchy. This surface area places it among the medium-scale Premier Crus of the commune, larger than some of the more intimate climats but smaller than the extensive holdings found in certain other Premier Cru designations within Santenay.

The vineyard's compact size contributes significantly to its terroir homogeneity, allowing for consistent soil composition and microclimate conditions throughout the climat. Unlike larger Premier Crus that may encompass various soil types and exposures, Clos Faubard's limited surface area ensures that vines across the entire vineyard experience remarkably similar growing conditions, contributing to the consistency and recognizable character of wines produced from this site.

The modest scale also means that annual production remains relatively limited, with typical yields producing between 800 to 1,200 cases annually, depending on vintage conditions and viticultural practices employed by the various proprietors. This limited production contributes to the vineyard's boutique character and helps maintain the exclusivity that Premier Cru status demands.

## Terroir & Geology

The geological foundation of Clos Faubard reveals the complex interplay of limestone, clay, and ancient marine sediments that characterize the finest sites of the Côte de Beaune. The vineyard sits atop Middle Jurassic formations, specifically the Bathonian stage limestones that form the backbone of Burgundy's greatest terroirs. These limestone deposits, laid down approximately 170 million years ago when the region lay beneath a warm, shallow sea, provide the mineral foundation that gives Clos Faubard wines their distinctive character.

The soil profile consists primarily of brown limestone soils with a notable clay component, creating the ideal balance between drainage and water retention that Pinot Noir demands. The topsoil layer, typically 40-60 centimeters deep, contains a mixture of decomposed limestone fragments, clay particles, and organic matter accumulated over centuries of careful viticulture. This relatively shallow topsoil layer forces vine roots to penetrate the underlying limestone bedrock, where they extract the mineral complexity that defines Premier Cru quality.

Clay content varies from 15-25% throughout the vineyard, with slightly higher concentrations in the lower sections where erosion has deposited finer particles over millennia. This clay component proves crucial for water retention during dry periods while maintaining sufficient drainage to prevent root rot during wet seasons. The limestone subsoil ensures excellent drainage while providing the mineral backbone that characterizes wines from this climat.

The vineyard's slope ranges from 8-12%, creating ideal conditions for both drainage and sun exposure while remaining gentle enough to prevent excessive erosion. This moderate gradient allows for optimal vine root development and ensures that each vine receives adequate sunlight exposure throughout the growing season. The southeastern aspect provides morning sun exposure that gently warms the vineyard after cool nights, while the afternoon positioning captures extended sunlight during the crucial ripening period.

Elevation ranges from approximately 280 to 320 meters above sea level, placing Clos Faubard in the sweet spot for Burgundian viticulture. This elevation provides sufficient altitude to benefit from cool nighttime temperatures that preserve acidity while remaining low enough to achieve full phenolic ripeness in most vintages.

## Climate & Microclimate

Clos Faubard benefits from a semi-continental climate characterized by warm summers, cool winters, and moderate rainfall distributed throughout the growing season. The vineyard's position within Santenay creates a unique microclimate that combines the general climatic patterns of the Côte de Beaune with site-specific influences that distinguish it from surrounding vineyards.

The southeastern exposure proves particularly advantageous, capturing the gentle morning sun that slowly warms the vineyard after cool nights, while avoiding the potentially harsh afternoon heat that can stress vines during hot summers. This orientation creates a natural temperature moderation system that extends the growing season and allows for gradual, even ripening of both Pinot Noir and Chardonnay.

The proximity to the Saône River valley, approximately 8 kilometers to the east, provides a moderating influence on temperature extremes. Cool air masses from the valley create beneficial nighttime cooling that preserves acidity in the grapes while warm daytime temperatures promote steady ripening. This diurnal temperature variation, often reaching 15-20°C during the growing season, proves crucial for developing the complex aromatics and maintaining the natural acidity that defines quality Burgundy.

Wind patterns within the vineyard are generally favorable, with gentle air circulation that reduces humidity and minimizes disease pressure. The natural bowl shape of Santenay's vineyard slopes creates air movement patterns that help prevent frost damage in spring and reduce moisture retention during humid periods.

Annual rainfall averages approximately 700-800mm, with distribution favoring spring growth and summer development while tapering during the crucial harvest period. The vineyard's excellent drainage ensures that excess water moves quickly through the soil profile, preventing waterlogging while maintaining adequate moisture reserves in the limestone subsoil.

## Viticulture

Clos Faubard is planted exclusively to Pinot Noir, reflecting Santenay's reputation as primarily a red wine appellation. The decision to focus solely on Pinot Noir in this climat stems from both historical precedent and the terroir's particular suitability for this noble variety.

Vine age across the vineyard varies considerably depending on the proprietor and parcel, with plantings ranging from recently established vines of 10-15 years to venerable old vines approaching 80 years of age. The most prized sections contain vines planted in the 1940s and 1950s, which have developed deep root systems that fully exploit the limestone terroir. These old vines typically produce lower yields but contribute exceptional concentration and complexity to the final wines.

Planting density follows traditional Burgundian practices, with approximately 10,000-12,000 vines per hectare. This high density encourages competition among vines, leading to deeper root development and more concentrated fruit production. The close spacing also maximizes the utilization of the precious Premier Cru terroir while maintaining the traditional landscape that has defined Burgundian viticulture for centuries.

Viticultural practices in Clos Faubard emphasize sustainable and often organic approaches, reflecting the modern understanding of terroir expression through minimal intervention. Most proprietors employ lutte raisonnée (reasoned struggle) methods, using treatments only when necessary and favoring biological solutions over synthetic chemicals. Soil cultivation typically involves careful plowing to maintain soil structure and encourage deep root development while avoiding excessive compaction.

Pruning follows the traditional Guyot system, with most vines trained to a single Guyot configuration that allows for optimal sun exposure and air circulation. Winter pruning is conducted with particular attention to vine balance, adjusting bud count based on vine age, vigor, and vintage conditions to optimize quality over quantity.

Harvest timing in Clos Faubard typically occurs in mid-to-late September, depending on vintage conditions. The southeastern exposure often allows for slightly earlier ripening compared to north-facing sites, but growers carefully monitor sugar accumulation, acid retention, and phenolic development to determine optimal picking dates for each parcel.

Yields are naturally regulated through careful vineyard management, with most quality-focused producers targeting 35-45 hectoliters per hectare. This yield restriction ensures concentration while maintaining the elegance that defines Santenay Premier Cru quality.

## Wine Character & Style

Wines from Clos Faubard display the refined elegance and mineral complexity that distinguish Premier Cru quality while maintaining the approachable charm that characterizes Santenay's finest expressions. The Pinot Noir produced from this climat exhibits a distinctive personality that reflects both its limestone terroir and its position within the southern Côte de Beaune.

The aromatic profile typically opens with vibrant red fruit characteristics, featuring cherry, raspberry, and strawberry notes that display remarkable purity and definition. As the wines develop, more complex secondary aromatics emerge, including subtle floral notes of violet and rose petal, complemented by gentle spice elements such as white pepper, cinnamon, and clove. The limestone influence manifests as a distinctive mineral undertone that adds depth and sophistication to the aromatic spectrum.

On the palate, Clos Faubard wines demonstrate excellent structure balanced by supple tannins that reflect the limestone terroir's influence. The texture is typically medium-bodied with silky tannins that provide framework without overwhelming the delicate fruit flavors. Acidity levels are well-integrated, providing freshness and aging potential while maintaining harmony with the wine's other components.

The flavor profile emphasizes pure red fruit expression, with cherry and raspberry dominating the mid-palate, supported by subtle earth tones and mineral notes that reflect the vineyard's geological foundation. Unlike the more powerful expressions found in northern Côte de Beaune sites, Clos Faubard wines display elegance and finesse, with flavors that unfold gradually rather than asserting immediate intensity.

The finish typically extends with mineral precision, displaying the limestone character that defines great Burgundy terroirs. Notes of crushed stone and wet earth complement the lingering fruit flavors, while gentle tannins provide structure that promises excellent aging potential.

What particularly distinguishes Clos Faubard from other Premier Crus is its ability to combine accessibility in youth with serious aging potential. The wines display charm and drinkability shortly after release while possessing the structural components necessary for extended cellar development.

## Comparison to Surrounding Crus

Within Santenay's Premier Cru hierarchy, Clos Faubard occupies a distinctive position that differentiates it from neighboring climats through both terroir characteristics and resulting wine styles. Compared to La Comme, another prestigious Santenay Premier Cru located on similar southeastern-facing slopes, Clos Faubard typically produces wines with greater mineral intensity and more pronounced limestone character, reflecting subtle differences in soil composition and elevation.

The wines from Clos Faubard display more refinement and elegance compared to those from Beaurepaire, a Premier Cru situated on steeper slopes with different geological characteristics. While Beaurepaire can produce more powerful and concentrated wines, Clos Faubard emphasizes finesse and aromatic complexity, making it particularly appealing to those who appreciate subtlety over power.

When compared to Gravières, perhaps Santenay's most famous Premier Cru, Clos Faubard wines show greater immediate approachability while maintaining serious aging potential. Gravières often requires more cellar time to reach peak drinking condition, while Clos Faubard provides pleasure across a broader drinking window.

The terroir differences between Clos Faubard and Les Clos Tavannes, another well-regarded Santenay Premier Cru, result in distinct stylistic variations. Clos Tavannes typically produces wines with more pronounced earth tones and rustic character, while Clos Faubard emphasizes pure fruit expression and mineral precision.

Compared to Premier Crus in neighboring Chassagne-Montrachet, particularly red wine sites like Morgeot or Clos Saint-Jean, Clos Faubard wines generally display less weight and concentration but offer greater elegance and aromatic purity. The Santenay terroir tends to produce wines with more immediate charm and accessibility compared to the often more structured and age-worthy expressions from Chassagne-Montrachet.

## Notable Producers

The limited size of Clos Faubard means that vineyard holdings are distributed among a select group of proprietors, each bringing their own viticultural and winemaking philosophy to this exceptional terroir. The fragmented ownership typical of Burgundy means that no single producer controls the entire vineyard, creating diversity in expression while maintaining the underlying terroir character that defines the climat.

Among the most respected producers working with Clos Faubard fruit, Domaine Louis Moreau has established a reputation for crafting wines that exemplify the vineyard's potential for elegance and complexity. Their approach emphasizes minimal intervention winemaking that allows the terroir to speak clearly, typically employing indigenous yeasts and moderate new oak usage to preserve the site's inherent character.

Domaine Vincent Bachelet maintains holdings within Clos Faubard that contribute to their reputation as one of Santenay's quality leaders. Their parcel, consisting of vines planted in the 1950s, produces wines of particular concentration and complexity, reflecting both the terroir potential and the benefits of old vine fruit.

Several négociant houses also work with Clos Faubard fruit, though the limited production means that these wines represent small portions of their overall portfolios. The challenge for négociants lies in maintaining the distinctive character of this specific climat while integrating it into broader production systems.

Stylistic variations among producers reflect different approaches to viticulture and winemaking rather than fundamental terroir differences. Some emphasize earlier harvest and lighter extraction to preserve aromatics and elegance, while others allow for longer hang time and more intensive extraction to maximize concentration and aging potential.

## Historical Background & Classification

The history of Clos Faubard extends back centuries, with documented viticulture in this specific site dating to at least the 15th century. Like many Burgundian climats, the vineyard name reflects its historical character, with "Clos" indicating its original enclosed nature and "Faubard" potentially derived from local toponymic references that have evolved over centuries of linguistic change.

The vineyard's classification as Premier Cru was officially established with the AOC laws of 1936, though its reputation for quality preceded formal recognition by several decades. Historical records from the 19th century indicate that wines from this site commanded premium prices compared to village-level Santenay, suggesting that quality recognition existed well before official classification.

The classification process considered several factors that contributed to Premier Cru status, including the vineyard's favorable exposition, optimal soil composition, and historical track record of producing wines superior to village-level appellations. The southeastern exposure and limestone-rich soils placed Clos Faubard among the most favored sites within Santenay's viticultural landscape.

During the phylloxera crisis of the late 19th century, Clos Faubard, like all Burgundian vineyards, required complete replanting with grafted vines. The recovery period during the early 20th century saw renewed attention to quality viticulture, setting the stage for the eventual Premier Cru classification.

The post-World War II period brought increased recognition of Santenay's Premier Crus, including Clos Faubard, as wine enthusiasts began to explore beyond the most famous communes of the Côte d'Or. This growing appreciation has continued into the modern era, with Clos Faubard gaining recognition among connoisseurs seeking high-quality Burgundy at more accessible price points than their northern counterparts.

## Aging Potential & Quality Level

Clos Faubard wines demonstrate excellent aging potential that reflects both the quality of the terroir and the structural characteristics imparted by limestone soils and careful viticulture. The aging trajectory typically follows a pattern that allows for enjoyment across multiple decades, though optimal drinking windows vary based on vintage conditions and winemaking approach.

In youth, typically during the first 2-3 years after vintage, Clos Faubard wines display vibrant fruit character and accessible tannins that make them appealing for early consumption. However, this early approachability should not be mistaken for lack of aging potential, as the wines possess the structural components necessary for extended development.

The optimal drinking window typically begins around 5-7 years after vintage, when the integration of tannins, acidity, and fruit flavors reaches ideal balance. During this period, the wines display the full complexity that Premier Cru terroir can provide, with primary fruit flavors evolving into more complex secondary and tertiary characteristics.

Peak maturity usually occurs between 8-15 years after vintage, depending on the specific vintage conditions and storage circumstances. During this phase, the limestone influence becomes more pronounced, while the wine develops the classic Burgundian characteristics of forest floor, mushroom, and subtle game notes that complement the evolved fruit flavors.

Well-stored examples can continue developing for 20-25 years, though the trajectory becomes more unpredictable in extended aging. The finest vintages from the best producers may reward even longer cellaring, developing the complex tertiary aromatics that represent the ultimate expression of aged Burgundy.

Quality consistency in Clos Faubard has improved significantly over recent decades as viticultural understanding has advanced and producers have focused increasingly on terroir expression. The vineyard's favorable terroir characteristics provide a solid foundation for quality even in challenging vintages, though the best expressions naturally occur during favorable growing seasons.

The overall quality level places Clos Faubard wines among the most serious expressions from Santenay, offering complexity and aging potential that rivals Premier Crus from more famous communes while maintaining the distinctive character that makes Santenay unique within the Burgundian hierarchy. For collectors and enthusiasts seeking authentic Premier Cru quality at reasonable prices, Clos Faubard represents one of Burgundy's most compelling opportunities.
