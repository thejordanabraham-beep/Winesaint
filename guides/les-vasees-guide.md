# Les Vasées Premier Cru: A Comprehensive Guide to Mercurey's Distinguished Climat

## Overview & Location

Les Vasées stands as one of Mercurey's most distinguished Premier Cru vineyards, representing the refined elegance that characterizes the finest expressions of the Côte Chalonnaise. Located in the southern portion of Burgundy's celebrated wine region, this climat occupies a privileged position within the Mercurey appellation, which itself serves as the largest and most prestigious commune of the Côte Chalonnaise.

The vineyard is strategically positioned on the hillsides east of the village of Mercurey, forming part of the commune's eastern amphitheater of vineyards that benefit from optimal solar exposure and protection from prevailing westerly winds. Les Vasées sits within a collection of Premier Cru sites that demonstrate the geological and climatic complexity that has made Mercurey renowned as the "jewel of the Côte Chalonnaise."

Within the broader context of Burgundy's hierarchy, Les Vasées represents the pinnacle of quality available in the Côte Chalonnaise, occupying the Premier Cru classification that places it above village-level Mercurey wines while maintaining the distinctive regional character that differentiates it from the more northerly Côte de Beaune and Côte de Nuits appellations. The climat's position within Mercurey's eastern-facing slopes provides it with the morning sunlight crucial for Pinot Noir ripening while benefiting from afternoon protection that helps preserve acidity and elegance.

## Size

Les Vasées encompasses approximately 4.2 hectares (10.4 acres) of vineyard land, making it a moderately-sized climat within Mercurey's Premier Cru hierarchy. This compact vineyard area allows for relatively consistent terroir expression across the site while providing sufficient production to maintain market presence among Burgundy enthusiasts.

The vineyard's modest size reflects the intimate scale typical of Burgundy's finest sites, where geological uniformity and microclimate consistency are prioritized over volume. The 4.2-hectare area is divided among several proprietors, with individual parcels ranging from small holdings of less than half a hectare to more substantial plots of one to two hectares, creating the mosaic of ownership that characterizes much of Burgundy's Premier Cru landscape.

## Terroir & Geology

The geological foundation of Les Vasées rests upon Middle Jurassic limestone formations characteristic of the Côte Chalonnaise, specifically the Bathonian and Bajocian stages that provide the mineral backbone essential for producing wines of Premier Cru quality. The bedrock consists primarily of hard limestone interspersed with marl deposits, creating a complex substrate that offers both drainage and water retention capabilities crucial for vine health and fruit quality.

The topsoil composition varies across the climat, with brown calcareous clay dominating the upper slopes and transitioning to deeper, more clay-rich soils in the lower sections. This variation in soil depth and composition (ranging from 40 to 80 centimeters of topsoil over limestone bedrock) creates subtle differences in vine vigor and fruit character across different parcels within the vineyard.

The slope characteristics of Les Vasées are particularly noteworthy, with gradients ranging from 8 to 15 percent that provide excellent drainage while preventing erosion. The vineyard faces primarily east to southeast, with aspects varying from 85 to 120 degrees, ensuring optimal morning sun exposure that facilitates gentle warming of the vines while avoiding the harsh afternoon heat that can compromise aromatic development in Pinot Noir.

Elevation within Les Vasées ranges from approximately 280 to 320 meters above sea level, positioning the vineyard within the ideal elevation band for Burgundian viticulture. This elevation provides sufficient altitude to benefit from diurnal temperature variation while remaining low enough to ensure consistent ripening in most vintages.

The limestone bedrock shows evidence of ancient marine deposits with fossil inclusions typical of Jurassic formations, contributing to the mineral complexity that characterizes wines from this site. Drainage patterns within the vineyard are enhanced by natural limestone fissures that allow excess water to percolate deeply, preventing waterlogging during wet periods while maintaining access to groundwater reserves during drought conditions.

## Climate & Microclimate

Les Vasées benefits from a semi-continental climate moderated by its position within the Saône Valley system, creating conditions that favor both aromatic development and physiological ripeness in Pinot Noir. The microclimate within this climat is influenced by several factors that distinguish it from surrounding vineyard sites.

The eastern and southeastern exposure provides crucial morning sunlight that initiates photosynthesis early in the day while the vineyard's position within a natural amphitheater offers protection from harsh western winds. This shelter effect creates a slightly warmer microclimate than fully exposed sites, typically adding 0.5 to 1.0 degrees Celsius to average growing season temperatures.

Diurnal temperature variation at Les Vasées typically ranges from 12 to 16 degrees Celsius during the growing season, with the limestone soils contributing to heat retention that moderates nighttime cooling. This temperature differential promotes anthocyanin development and aromatic complexity while preserving natural acidity levels essential for wine balance.

The site experiences approximately 1,350 to 1,400 hours of sunshine during the growing season, with morning fog occurrence averaging 15-20 days annually. These fog events, typically occurring in late summer and early autumn, can contribute to beneficial concentration effects when followed by sunny, breezy conditions.

Precipitation patterns show Les Vasées receiving slightly less rainfall than higher elevation sites within Mercurey, averaging 650-700mm annually, with the vineyard's excellent drainage ensuring that excess moisture rarely poses problems for vine health or fruit quality. The microclimate's relative warmth typically advances phenological development by 3-5 days compared to cooler sites within the commune.

## Viticulture

Les Vasées is planted exclusively to Pinot Noir, reflecting the climat's particular suitability for this noble Burgundian variety. The vineyard's exposition, soil composition, and microclimate characteristics align ideally with Pinot Noir's requirements for producing wines of Premier Cru caliber.

Vine age across the climat varies considerably among different proprietors and parcels, with plantings ranging from young vines of 15-20 years to venerable stocks approaching 60-70 years of age. The majority of the vineyard consists of vines between 25-45 years old, representing the optimal balance between vigor and concentration that produces the most complex and balanced wines.

Planting density within Les Vasées follows traditional Burgundian practices, typically ranging from 9,000 to 10,500 vines per hectare. This high-density planting encourages competition among vines while ensuring optimal canopy management and fruit concentration. Row orientation generally follows the natural slope contours, facilitating both vineyard work and erosion prevention.

Rootstock selection varies among producers but commonly includes SO4, 161-49C, and Riparia Gloire, chosen for their compatibility with the limestone-rich soils and their ability to regulate vine vigor appropriately for the site's characteristics. Clonal selection typically emphasizes Pinot Noir clones 115, 667, and 777, often supplemented with selections massales from older vineyard blocks.

Viticultural practices within Les Vasées emphasize sustainable approaches that respect the site's natural characteristics. Soil management typically involves light cultivation or cover crop usage, with many producers adopting organic or biodynamic practices. Canopy management focuses on achieving optimal leaf-to-fruit ratios through careful pruning, shoot positioning, and selective leaf removal.

Harvest timing at Les Vasées typically occurs in mid to late September, with the climat's favorable microclimate often allowing for extended hang time that enhances phenolic development. Yields are generally controlled through winter pruning and green harvesting, with most producers targeting 35-42 hectoliters per hectare to ensure concentration and quality.

Site-specific viticultural challenges include managing vine vigor in the deeper, more fertile soils of lower slope areas and ensuring adequate water stress in particularly wet vintages. The limestone bedrock can occasionally create challenges for new plantings, requiring careful soil preparation and root zone management.

## Wine Character & Style

Wines from Les Vasées exemplify the refined elegance and mineral precision that characterize Mercurey's finest Premier Crus. The climat produces Pinot Noirs that display a distinctive combination of red fruit purity, floral complexity, and mineral backbone that reflects the limestone-rich terroir.

The aromatic profile typically features primary notes of red cherry, raspberry, and strawberry, often accompanied by violet and rose petal nuances that develop during fermentation and early élevage. Secondary aromatics frequently include subtle spice elements (particularly white pepper and cinnamon) along with earthy undertones of forest floor and subtle mineral notes reminiscent of wet limestone.

On the palate, Les Vasées Pinot Noirs demonstrate medium body with elegant structure characterized by fine-grained tannins that provide support without overwhelming the fruit expression. The limestone influence manifests as a distinctive mineral tension that runs through the wine's mid-palate, contributing to both complexity and aging potential.

Acidity levels typically range from 3.2 to 3.5 pH, providing the backbone necessary for food compatibility and cellaring potential while maintaining the approachable character that makes these wines appealing in their youth. Alcohol levels generally fall between 12.5% and 13.5%, reflecting the climat's ability to achieve phenolic ripeness without excessive sugar accumulation.

The textural qualities of Les Vasées wines are particularly noteworthy, displaying a silky mouthfeel with subtle grip that evolves gracefully with bottle age. Young wines often show bright fruit intensity with firm but approachable tannins, while mature examples develop tertiary complexities including leather, truffle, and dried herb characteristics.

Color intensity varies with vintage conditions and winemaking approaches but typically shows medium ruby depth with good transparency, developing brick and garnet hues with extended aging. The wines demonstrate excellent clarity and brilliance, reflecting both terroir characteristics and careful vinification practices.

What particularly distinguishes Les Vasées wines is their combination of immediate appeal and long-term development potential, offering both accessible fruit expression and the structural complexity that rewards patience. The mineral finish, often described as chalky or limestone-driven, provides a signature characteristic that marks these wines as products of their specific terroir.

## Comparison to Surrounding Crus

Within Mercurey's Premier Cru hierarchy, Les Vasées occupies a distinctive position that showcases both similarities to and differences from neighboring climats. Compared to the more robust and structured wines of Clos du Roy, Les Vasées typically displays greater elegance and finesse, with more pronounced floral characteristics and a lighter touch overall.

The neighboring Premier Cru of Les Montaigus produces wines with similar mineral precision but often shows more overt earthiness and slightly firmer tannic structure, reflecting differences in soil composition and microclimate exposure. Les Vasées wines typically demonstrate more immediate accessibility while maintaining comparable aging potential.

When compared to Clos des Myglands, another prestigious Mercurey Premier Cru, Les Vasées shows more consistent elegance across different vintages, while Clos des Myglands can produce more powerful wines in exceptional years but sometimes lacks the consistent refinement that characterizes Les Vasées.

The Premier Cru of Sazenay, located on similar eastern-facing slopes, produces wines with comparable structure but often displays more pronounced spice characteristics and slightly higher natural acidity. Les Vasées wines typically show more developed floral complexity and softer tannin integration.

Relative to village-level Mercurey wines, Les Vasées demonstrates significantly greater complexity, concentration, and aging potential. The Premier Cru designation reflects not only superior terroir characteristics but also more restrictive viticultural practices that enhance quality and distinctiveness.

Within the broader Côte Chalonnaise context, Les Vasées ranks among the most refined and elegant red wine productions, comparing favorably to top Premier Crus from Givry and Rully while maintaining a distinctive personality that reflects Mercurey's particular terroir characteristics.

## Historical Background & Classification

The history of Les Vasées as a recognized vineyard site extends back several centuries, with documented wine production dating to at least the 16th century when monastic communities first established systematic viticulture in the Mercurey region. The climat's name likely derives from local dialect references to muddy or clay-rich areas, reflecting the soil characteristics that distinguish this site.

Classification as a Premier Cru occurred as part of Burgundy's comprehensive appellation system development in the mid-20th century, with Les Vasées officially receiving Premier Cru status in 1943 along with other distinguished Mercurey vineyard sites. This classification recognized the climat's demonstrated ability to produce wines of superior quality and distinctive character compared to village-level sites.

Historical records from the 18th and 19th centuries indicate that wines from Les Vasées commanded premium prices in regional markets, suggesting early recognition of the site's quality potential. The climat's reputation was further enhanced during the phylloxera reconstruction period when careful replanting emphasized quality over quantity.

The development of Les Vasées' modern reputation coincided with the general elevation of Côte Chalonnaise wines in the latter half of the 20th century, as wine enthusiasts and critics began to recognize the region's potential for producing wines of Premier Cru caliber at more accessible prices than their Côte d'Or counterparts.

Documentation from the 1970s and 1980s shows increasing interest from quality-focused producers who recognized Les Vasées' potential for expressing distinctive terroir characteristics. This period saw improvements in viticultural practices and winemaking techniques that helped establish the climat's current reputation for consistent quality and distinctive character.

## Aging Potential & Quality Level

Les Vasées wines demonstrate impressive aging potential that reflects both the climat's terroir characteristics and the inherent longevity of well-made Burgundian Pinot Noir. Young wines typically show excellent fruit purity and accessibility but benefit significantly from 3-5 years of cellaring to allow tannin integration and secondary character development.

The optimal drinking window for Les Vasées wines generally extends from 5-15 years after vintage, with exceptional examples from outstanding vintages capable of graceful evolution for 20 years or more. The limestone influence provides the mineral backbone necessary for extended aging while the elegant tannin structure ensures that wines remain approachable throughout their development.

Quality consistency across different vintages is notably high for Les Vasées, with the climat's favorable microclimate and excellent drainage helping to minimize vintage variation effects. Even in challenging years, wines from this site typically maintain varietal character and structural integrity, though concentration and complexity may vary.

The evolution trajectory for Les Vasées wines typically shows primary fruit characteristics gradually giving way to secondary and tertiary complexities while maintaining the mineral precision that characterizes the site. Young wines display bright red fruit and floral notes, while mature examples develop earthy, truffle, and dried herb characteristics without losing their essential elegance.

Temperature and humidity control during aging are particularly important for Les Vasées wines, as their elegant structure benefits from stable cellar conditions that allow gradual development. Properly stored bottles show excellent color retention and aromatic evolution, with tannins becoming increasingly integrated while maintaining structural support.

The quality level achieved by Les Vasées consistently places it among Mercurey's top Premier Cru sites, with wines offering complexity and character that justify their classification while providing exceptional value compared to similar-quality sites in the Côte d'Or. This combination of quality, distinctiveness, and relative accessibility has established Les Vasées as a reference point for understanding both Mercurey's potential and the broader character of Côte Chalonnaise terroir.
