# Clos des Perrières: Premier Cru of Meursault

## Overview & Location

Clos des Perrières stands as one of Meursault's most distinguished Premier Cru vineyards, occupying a privileged position on the upper slopes of this celebrated commune in Burgundy's Côte de Beaune. Located in the southern section of the Côte d'Or, approximately 15 kilometers southwest of Beaune, this climat represents the pinnacle of white Burgundy production within Meursault's hierarchy of vineyards.

The vineyard sits strategically positioned on the hillside above the village of Meursault, nestled between the communes of Volnay to the north and Puligny-Montrachet to the south. This location places Clos des Perrières within the heart of Burgundy's most prestigious white wine territory, where Chardonnay achieves extraordinary expressions of terroir. The climat occupies the upper portion of Meursault's Premier Cru vineyard band, positioned at an elevation that provides optimal exposure while maintaining the mineral complexity that defines great Burgundian Chardonnay.

Within the broader context of the Côte de Beaune, Clos des Perrières benefits from the geological and climatic conditions that have made this slope one of the world's most celebrated wine regions. The vineyard's position on the eastern face of the Côte allows it to capture the morning sun while being protected from harsh western winds, creating an ideal microenvironment for Chardonnay cultivation.

## Size

Clos des Perrières encompasses 16.47 hectares (40.68 acres), making it one of the larger Premier Cru vineyards in Meursault. This substantial size allows for meaningful production while maintaining the site's distinctive character across its extent. The vineyard's dimensions provide sufficient scale for multiple producers to hold parcels within the climat, each contributing their interpretation of this exceptional terroir.

The vineyard's boundaries are clearly delineated, following the traditional Burgundian concept of a "clos" or enclosed vineyard, though the physical walls that once defined many such sites have largely disappeared over the centuries. The climat's borders are defined by subtle but significant changes in soil composition, slope angle, and exposure that distinguish Clos des Perrières from its neighboring vineyards.

## Terroir & Geology

The geological foundation of Clos des Perrières consists primarily of Bathonian limestone from the Middle Jurassic period, overlain with varying depths of clay and limestone-rich soils. This limestone base provides the mineral backbone that characterizes the finest white Burgundies, while the clay content varies across the vineyard, creating subtle differences in wine character from different parcels.

The soil composition features a complex mixture of white and brown limestone fragments, clay, and decomposed limestone debris known locally as "chailles." The topsoil depth ranges from 30 to 60 centimeters, with the limestone bedrock lying relatively close to the surface in the upper sections of the vineyard. This shallow soil profile forces vine roots to penetrate the limestone substrate, accessing the mineral reserves that contribute to the wine's distinctive character.

The vineyard displays a gentle to moderate easterly slope, with gradients ranging from 5 to 12 degrees. This inclination provides excellent drainage while ensuring optimal sun exposure throughout the growing season. The upper portions of the climat feature stonier soils with higher limestone content, while the lower sections contain more clay, creating natural zones within the vineyard that produce wines with subtly different characteristics.

Drainage characteristics are exceptional throughout Clos des Perrières, with the combination of slope and permeable limestone substrate preventing water accumulation while maintaining sufficient moisture retention during dry periods. The limestone's natural filtration properties ensure that excess rainfall quickly drains away, while the clay component provides water reserves during summer drought conditions.

## Climate & Microclimate

Clos des Perrières benefits from a continental climate modified by oceanic influences, typical of the Côte de Beaune but with specific microclimate characteristics created by its topographical position. The vineyard's elevation, ranging from approximately 240 to 280 meters above sea level, creates a cooling effect that extends the growing season and promotes the development of complex aromatics while maintaining natural acidity.

The eastern exposure provides optimal morning sun exposure, allowing for gradual warming of the vineyard throughout the day. This orientation protects the vines from the potentially damaging effects of late afternoon heat while ensuring adequate photosynthesis and sugar accumulation. The hillside position creates natural air circulation patterns that reduce humidity and minimize disease pressure, particularly important for Chardonnay's susceptibility to fungal infections.

Temperature variations within the climat are influenced by elevation and soil composition. The upper sections, with their stonier soils and higher elevation, experience slightly cooler conditions that promote mineral expression and acidity retention. The lower portions, with deeper clay soils, retain heat more effectively, contributing to fuller body and richness in the resulting wines.

The microclimate of Clos des Perrières is further influenced by its position within the broader Meursault amphitheater of vineyards. The surrounding hills create a protective environment that moderates temperature extremes while promoting the steady ripening conditions essential for producing wines of elegance and complexity.

## Viticulture

Chardonnay is the exclusive grape variety planted in Clos des Perrières, as mandated by appellation regulations for Meursault Premier Cru vineyards. The vines are typically planted at densities ranging from 10,000 to 12,000 plants per hectare, following traditional Burgundian practices that promote competition among vines and concentrate flavors in the grapes.

Average vine age across the climat varies significantly among different producers, with some parcels containing vines over 50 years old while others have been replanted more recently. The older vines, particularly those planted in the 1960s and 1970s, produce grapes with exceptional concentration and complexity, their deep root systems having established intimate contact with the limestone bedrock.

Viticultural practices in Clos des Perrières generally follow sustainable or organic principles, with many producers adopting environmentally conscious approaches. Canopy management is crucial given Chardonnay's vigorous growth habits, requiring careful leaf removal and shoot positioning to optimize sun exposure and air circulation. The timing and extent of these operations vary depending on the specific microclimate conditions of individual parcels within the climat.

Soil management practices emphasize maintaining the natural structure and biological activity of the vineyard's unique terroir. Many producers employ minimal intervention approaches, avoiding chemical fertilizers in favor of organic compost and careful cultivation techniques that preserve the soil's natural characteristics. The shallow topsoil and underlying limestone require careful machinery operation to prevent soil compaction and damage to the vine root systems.

Harvest timing in Clos des Perrières typically occurs in mid to late September, depending on vintage conditions and individual producer preferences. The vineyard's elevation and exposure can create variations in ripening patterns, with some sections reaching optimal maturity several days before others, allowing producers to harvest different parcels at peak conditions.

## Wine Character & Style

Wines from Clos des Perrières display a distinctive character that combines power with elegance, representing the apex of Meursault's expression. The aromatics typically feature intense mineral notes complemented by white flower essences, particularly acacia and hawthorn, along with subtle citrus elements of lemon zest and white grapefruit. As the wines mature, they develop complex tertiary aromatics including honey, brioche, and hazelnut notes that reflect both the terroir's influence and careful winemaking.

The palate demonstrates remarkable mineral precision, with a chalky, limestone-driven texture that provides structure and length. The wine's body ranges from medium to full, with a distinctive oily richness balanced by vibrant acidity that reflects the site's elevation and limestone soils. The mineral backbone provides a framework for layered flavors that unfold gradually, revealing white fruit characteristics of pear and apple, complemented by subtle tropical notes in warmer vintages.

Textural qualities are particularly noteworthy in Clos des Perrières wines, displaying a combination of richness and tension that defines great white Burgundy. The mouthfeel often exhibits a creamy, almost viscous quality derived from extended lees contact during élevage, while maintaining the nervous energy that keeps the wine fresh and age-worthy. The limestone influence creates a distinctive chalky minerality that coats the palate and extends through the wine's remarkably long finish.

The wines demonstrate exceptional complexity, with multiple layers of flavor and aroma that evolve continuously in the glass. Young wines may appear somewhat closed, requiring time to reveal their full potential, while properly cellared bottles develop extraordinary depth and sophistication over decades. The site's unique terroir imparts a signature salinity and mineral precision that distinguishes Clos des Perrières from other Premier Cru sites in Meursault.

## Comparison to Surrounding Crus

Within Meursault's Premier Cru hierarchy, Clos des Perrières occupies a position of particular distinction, producing wines that display greater mineral intensity and aging potential than most neighboring sites. Compared to Les Perrières, its immediate neighbor, Clos des Perrières tends to produce wines with more structure and longevity, though both share similar limestone-driven minerality.

The wines differ markedly from those of Les Genevrières, another prestigious Meursault Premier Cru, which typically displays more immediate charm and rounder fruit character. Clos des Perrières maintains greater austerity in youth, requiring extended cellaring to reveal its full complexity. This structural backbone places it closer in style to the great white wines of Puligny-Montrachet, though with Meursault's characteristic richness and power.

When compared to Les Charmes, perhaps Meursault's most famous Premier Cru, Clos des Perrières shows greater mineral precision and less opulent fruit character. While Charmes wines often display seductive immediate appeal, Clos des Perrières demands patience, rewarding those who allow the wine to develop its full potential over time.

The elevation and limestone content of Clos des Perrières create wines with more pronounced mineral characteristics than the lower-elevation Premier Crus of Meursault. Sites such as Le Porusot and Les Santenots, while producing excellent wines, typically show more clay influence and correspondingly richer, more accessible styles compared to the aristocratic reserve of Clos des Perrières.

## Notable Producers

Several distinguished producers own parcels within Clos des Perrières, each bringing their interpretation to this exceptional terroir. Domaine des Comtes Lafon holds significant holdings within the climat and is widely recognized for producing benchmark expressions that capture the site's mineral complexity and aging potential. Their approach emphasizes minimal intervention winemaking and extended lees aging to showcase the terroir's natural characteristics.

Albert Grivault, with substantial holdings in the climat, produces wines that demonstrate the site's capacity for both power and elegance. Their traditional approach to viticulture and winemaking results in wines that require extended cellaring but reward patience with extraordinary complexity and longevity.

Domaine Roulot's parcels in Clos des Perrières produce wines that showcase the producer's commitment to expressing pure terroir through meticulous vineyard management and careful winemaking. Their bottles consistently demonstrate the site's distinctive mineral signature while maintaining Meursault's characteristic richness.

Other notable producers include Domaine Jobard, whose traditional approach results in wines of remarkable longevity and classical expression, and Domaine Boyer-Martenot, which produces more accessible interpretations while respecting the site's fundamental character. Each producer's specific parcel location within the climat influences their wine's character, with those holding vines in the upper, stonier sections typically producing more mineral-driven wines.

## Historical Background & Classification

The history of Clos des Perrières extends back centuries, with viticultural activities documented in the area since medieval times. The name "Perrières" derives from the French word for stone quarries, referencing the historical extraction of limestone from the hillsides that created the terraced landscape still visible today. This geological activity inadvertently improved the vineyard's drainage and exposed the mineral-rich subsoils that contribute to the wine's distinctive character.

The climat achieved Premier Cru classification in 1937 when the French appellation system formally recognized Burgundy's hierarchical vineyard classification. However, the site's reputation for producing exceptional wines was established long before official recognition, with historical records indicating premium prices for wines from this location dating back to the 18th century.

Throughout the 19th century, Clos des Perrières wines commanded prices comparable to the finest white wines of the Côte de Beaune, establishing the site's commercial reputation alongside its qualitative recognition. The vineyard survived the phylloxera crisis of the late 19th century through replanting with grafted vines, maintaining its productive capacity while preserving the essential character that had made its wines famous.

The modern era has seen continued recognition of Clos des Perrières' exceptional quality, with wine critics and collectors acknowledging its position among Burgundy's finest white wine sites. The climat's reputation has grown internationally as understanding of Burgundian terroir has deepened, cementing its status as one of Meursault's most prestigious Premier Cru vineyards.

## Aging Potential & Quality Level

Wines from Clos des Perrières demonstrate exceptional aging potential, with properly stored bottles capable of evolution over two to three decades or more. The site's limestone-driven structure and natural acidity provide the framework necessary for extended cellaring, while the wine's complexity continues to develop throughout its aging trajectory.

Young wines from the climat often appear somewhat austere, with pronounced mineral character dominating the fruit expression. This initial reserve is characteristic of great white Burgundy terroirs and indicates the wine's potential for long-term development. During the first five years after vintage, the wines gradually integrate their components while beginning to show the complexity that distinguishes Premier Cru sites.

The optimal drinking window typically begins around seven to ten years after vintage, when the wine achieves balance between its mineral structure and developed aromatics. At this stage, Clos des Perrières wines display their full range of characteristics, combining power with elegance in expressions that justify the site's prestigious reputation.

Peak maturity often occurs between ten and twenty years after vintage, depending on storage conditions and specific vintage characteristics. During this period, the wines achieve extraordinary complexity, with tertiary aromatics fully developed while maintaining the freshness and mineral precision that defines the site. The finest examples can continue evolving positively for additional decades.

Quality consistency across vintages is generally high, though the site's expression varies with climatic conditions. Warmer years tend to produce more immediately accessible wines with enhanced tropical fruit notes, while cooler vintages emphasize the mineral structure and require extended cellaring. The terroir's fundamental characteristics remain constant across different weather patterns, ensuring that each vintage expresses the distinctive personality of Clos des Perrières while reflecting the specific growing season's conditions.
