## The Barolo MGA System

To provide context: In 2010, Barolo introduced an official system of sub-zones called Menzioni Geografiche Aggiuntive (MGA or MeGA), which are official additional geographical indications within the Barolo DOCG rules. These sub-zones include:

- Entire villages (such as La Morra)
- Specified single vineyards (such as Bussia or Cannubi)
- Groups of specified vineyards consolidated into a single MGA (for example, the Via Nuova vineyard was included within the Terlo MGA)

The most prestigious and well-documented MGAs in Barolo's written and oral traditions include sites such as Rocche dell'Annunziata, Brunate, Cerequio in La Morra (some spilling into Barolo village), Cannubi and Sarmazza in Barolo village, and Rocche di Castiglione in Castiglione Falletto, among others.
