## Context: The MGA System in Barolo

To provide useful context, the Menzioni Geografiche Aggiuntive system was officially introduced in 2010 within the Barolo DOCG regulations. This classification system recognizes three types of sub-zones:

- **Entire villages** (such as La Morra)
- **Specified single vineyards** (including prestigious sites like Bussia and Cannubi)
- **Multiple vineyards consolidated into a single MGA** (for example, the Via Nuova vineyard was incorporated into the Terlo MGA)

The system formalized what had long been recognized in both written tradition (from Lorenzo Fantini in the late 19th century through modern writers like Luigi Veronelli, Renato Ratti, and Alessandro Masnaghetti) and in local oral tradition: certain privileged positions in Barolo produce more distinctive wines and command higher prices.

## Documented Major Barolo Crus

For reference, the most consistently celebrated crus in Barolo literature include:

- **In La Morra:** Rocche dell'Annunziata, Brunate, and Cerequio (the latter two spilling into the village of Barolo)
- **In Barolo village:** Cannubi and Sarmazza
- **In Castiglione Falletto:** Rocche di Castiglione
- **Other notable sites:** Vigna Rionda

If Bablino is indeed a legitimate MGA, it would be among the lesser-known designations that have yet to achieve significant documentation in the major reference works on the region, including Alessandro Masnaghetti's comprehensive "Barolo MGA: The Barolo Great Vineyards Encyclopedia" (2015) and Kerin O'Keefe's "Barolo and Barbaresco" (2014).
